## 1. Beyond oncogenesis: the unexplored benefits of viruses in cancer immunity — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Reviews Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-18
- **Authors**: Wang XW et al.
- **Aims**: Reframe virome-cancer interactions beyond viral oncogenesis to explore how non-oncogenic viruses enhance antitumour immunity.
- **Method**: Narrative/systematic review integrating animal-host coevolution data, virome composition studies, and immunological evidence across cancer types.
- **Key findings**: Non-oncogenic viruses prime innate/adaptive immunity and mimic tumour antigens; the enterovirus/rhinovirus CE1 epitope is associated with protective liver cancer immunity. Endogenous retroviruses, bacteriophages, and oncolytic viruses may act as anticancer allies.
- **Strengths**: Top-tier journal, NCI-led authorship, synthesises vast cross-disciplinary evidence into a novel virome-guided cancer-prevention framework.
- **Limitations**: Review-level evidence; causal virome–immunity links remain to be established in prospective cohorts.
- **Conclusion**: A virome-guided framework could reshape cancer risk assessment, immune therapeutics, and prevention especially in low-resource settings.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41568-026-00948-6 / PMID:42315907

## 2. Recurrent intra-tumour heterogeneity is a hallmark of metastatic prostate cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-19
- **Authors**: Weng S et al.
- **Aims**: Define mechanisms underpinning transcriptional and genomic heterogeneity across metastatic lesions in late-stage prostate cancer.
- **Method**: Single-cell multi-omics and whole-genome sequencing of 34 metastatic lesions from 9 mCRPC patients via rapid autopsy.
- **Key findings**: Functional intra-tumour subpopulations converge recurrently across metastases irrespective of clonal background; microenvironment drives co-adaptation but not transcriptional heterogeneity per se.
- **Strengths**: Exceptional depth (34 lesions, 9 patients), rapid-autopsy design, integrative multi-omics.
- **Limitations**: Small patient N=9 limits generalisability; single institution.
- **Conclusion**: System-level selection pressures drive recurrent functional convergence of mCRPC ecosystems, identifying new therapeutic targets.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74334-z / PMID:42321191

## 3. Benchmark of biomarker identification and prognostic modeling methods on diverse censored data — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-16
- **Authors**: Fletcher W et al.
- **Aims**: Compare variable-selection and survival-prediction methods for high-dimensional genomic cancer data with right-censored outcomes.
- **Method**: Simulation study across diverse synthetic datasets (varying sparsity, correlation, signal strength) plus validation on TCGA cancer cohort; metrics include FDR, F1, C-index, Brier score, RMSE.
- **Key findings**: CoxBoost and Adaptive LASSO excelled across all metrics; LASSO and elastic net led on C-index and F1; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR control was volatile under correlated predictors.
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive multi-metric comparison, varied simulation scenarios, open TCGA validation; directly actionable guidance for cancer genomic epi.
- **Limitations**: Synthetic-data focus; real-world batch effects and missing data not modelled.
- **Conclusion**: CoxBoost and Adaptive LASSO are best-in-class for biomarker discovery and prognosis in high-dimensional censored cancer data.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0351429 / PMID:42302057

## 4. Spatio-temporal changes in clusters of gastric cancer incidence: The impact of nationwide cancer control programs in South Korea — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-16
- **Authors**: Kim JH et al.
- **Aims**: Identify shifts in high- and low-risk spatial clusters of gastric cancer incidence before and after South Korea's nationwide prevention programs.
- **Method**: Ecological study using national cancer registry across 243 districts; Local Moran's I and Getis-Ord Gi* spatial clustering compared between 2009–2013 and 2014–2018.
- **Key findings**: Low-risk districts expanded markedly (26→37) and high-risk districts contracted slightly (31→28); post-program low-risk areas showed more green space, walking, and lower obesity (p<0.002).
- **Strengths**: National registry data, two validated spatial methods, large geographic coverage.
- **Limitations**: Ecological design precludes individual-level causal inference; confounders at district level not fully controlled.
- **Conclusion**: Lifestyle- and environment-focused prevention may drive gastric cancer cluster shifts; locally tailored strategies recommended.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0349384 / PMID:42302017
