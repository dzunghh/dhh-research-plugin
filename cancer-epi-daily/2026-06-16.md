## 1. Prostate Imaging Standards for Screening Magnetic Resonance Imaging (PRISM): International Consensus Recommendations — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Mayor N et al.
- **Aims**: To provide international consensus recommendations on MRI acquisition, interpretation, and reporting for prostate cancer screening programs.
- **Method**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 6 RCTs/prospective cohorts (n=1,919), followed by RAND/UCLA Appropriateness Method Delphi consensus with 21 international experts.
- **Key findings**: Pooled screening MRI biopsy recommendation rate was 19.2%; grade group ≥2 cancer detection was 6.0%. Among 323 consensus statements, 235 (72.8%) reached agreement.
- **Strengths**: Rigorous two-stage design combining quantitative evidence synthesis with structured expert consensus; multinational panel spanning 6 countries.
- **Limitations**: Few eligible screening studies (n=6); heterogeneity in PSA threshold criteria precluded consensus on optimal cutoff.
- **Conclusion**: PRISM recommendations standardize abbreviated non-contrast MRI protocols, stage-gated reporting, and quality assurance standards for prostate cancer screening.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1711 / PMID:42275039

## 2. The Role of Gastric Atrophy in the Association Between Helicobacter pylori Infection and Esophageal Adenocarcinoma — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Zhang J et al.
- **Aims**: To quantify the mediation role of gastric atrophy, BMI, and GERD in the inverse association between H. pylori and esophageal adenocarcinoma (EAC).
- **Method**: Multicenter case-control study (435 EAC cases, 1,298 controls; BEACON consortium); 4-way effect decomposition for causal mediation analysis.
- **Key findings**: Seropositivity for all 15 H. pylori antigens was inversely associated with EAC (ORs 0.44–0.91). Natural indirect effects through gastric atrophy, BMI, and GERD were each near zero.
- **Strengths**: Large multicenter consortium; novel application of 4-way decomposition allows simultaneous mediation and interaction quantification.
- **Limitations**: Serum samples collected 1993–2004 limit generalizability; cross-sectional biomarker measurement may not reflect lifetime exposure.
- **Conclusion**: The H. pylori–EAC inverse association is not mediated by gastric atrophy or classic reflux-related pathways, warranting investigation of alternative mechanisms.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-1312 / PMID:42274332

## 3. PEPRVision: A Visualization Framework for Pharmacoepidemiology Research — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Am J Epidemiol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Wang Y et al.
- **Aims**: To introduce PEPRVision, a visualization framework transforming structured longitudinal healthcare data into intuitive patient-profile graphics for pharmacoepidemiology.
- **Method**: Framework development applying preattentive visual processing principles, demonstrated via 4 US administrative claims case studies (algorithm validation, reverse causation, teratogenicity, prenatal exposure).
- **Key findings**: PEPRVision facilitated identification of reverse causation and measurement errors in claims data; visual profiles complement population-level causal inference studies.
- **Strengths**: Addresses a practical gap in pharmacoepidemiology tooling; multiple case studies demonstrate versatility across research questions.
- **Limitations**: Framework validation is qualitative; no formal comparison of error detection rates versus standard tabular review.
- **Conclusion**: PEPRVision enhances validity and clinical relevance of pharmacoepidemiology by enabling rapid visual pattern recognition in longitudinal claims data.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/aje/kwag121 / PMID:42272141

## 4. World Cancer Research Fund/American Institute for Cancer Research Screener Algorithm Versus Standard Score: Association with Mortality in Colorectal Cancer Patients — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Nafría M et al.
- **Aims**: To evaluate the external validity of the WCRF/AICR Screener algorithm for cancer prevention recommendations and its association with all-cause mortality in colorectal cancer (CRC) survivors.
- **Method**: Prospective Dutch COLON cohort (n=1,919 stage I–IV CRC patients); Cox proportional hazards models with median 8.3-year follow-up; leave-one-out variable contribution analysis.
- **Key findings**: Highest versus lowest Screener quartile associated with lower all-cause mortality (HR 0.73, 95% CI 0.57–0.93); comparable to standard Score (HR 0.82, 95% CI 0.64–1.05).
- **Strengths**: Large prospective cohort with long follow-up; direct head-to-head comparison of brief vs. full assessment tools.
- **Limitations**: Single-country cohort limits generalizability; Screener developed for a different population and setting.
- **Conclusion**: The WCRF/AICR Screener shows comparable prognostic performance to the full Score, supporting its use in clinical survivorship care.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-2056 / PMID:42274335

## 5. Longitudinal Association of Quantitative Background Parenchymal Enhancement with Breast Cancer Risk Among Women with BRCA1/2 Pathogenic Variants — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-10
- **Authors**: Nguyen AA et al.
- **Aims**: To investigate the longitudinal association of quantitative MRI background parenchymal enhancement (BPE) with breast cancer risk in BRCA1/2 pathogenic variant carriers.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort (n=385 women; 1,278 serial MRIs); repeated-measures proportional hazards models adjusted for age, menopause, volumetric breast density, BMI, and BRCA type.
- **Key findings**: BPE20/FGT ratio associated with increased breast cancer risk (HR 1.13 per 10 units, p<0.005); association stronger in axial MRIs (HR 1.34). BPE decreased post-oophorectomy (p<0.04).
- **Strengths**: Serial imaging design captures within-person BPE change over time; oophorectomy sub-analysis provides mechanistic insight.
- **Limitations**: Relatively small sample (39 events); single-institution data may limit generalizability.
- **Conclusion**: Quantitative BPE may refine breast cancer risk stratification in BRCA1/2 carriers and inform risk-reducing intervention decisions.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-1952 / PMID:42268243
