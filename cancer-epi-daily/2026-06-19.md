## 1. Aumolertinib with or without chemotherapy in EGFR-mutated advanced non-small-cell lung cancer (AENEAS2): an open-label, multicentre, randomised, controlled, phase 3 trial — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-15
- **Authors**: Li Z et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate aumolertinib + platinum-based chemotherapy vs aumolertinib monotherapy as first-line therapy in EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC.
- **Method**: Open-label multicentre phase 3 RCT; 624 patients at 60 China hospitals; 1:1 randomisation stratified by EGFR mutation and brain metastasis; BICR-assessed PFS (RECIST v1.1).
- **Key findings**: Median PFS 28.9 vs 18.9 months (HR 0.47, 95% CI 0.37–0.60, p<0.0001); grade 3–4 neutropenia 55% vs 1% with combination.
- **Strengths**: Prospective stratification, blinded central radiological review, large multinational cancer centre sample.
- **Limitations**: China-only enrolment limits generalisability; OS data immature; open-label design risks ascertainment bias.
- **Conclusion**: Aumolertinib + chemotherapy delivers a clinically meaningful PFS benefit in first-line EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC despite increased haematologic toxicity.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00090-2 / PMID:42296979

## 2. Radiation risk of screening-detected diffuse goiter in a Belarusian cohort exposed as children or adolescents to iodine-131 after Chernobyl — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-16
- **Authors**: Zablotska LB et al.
- **Aims**: Quantify the dose-response relationship between childhood iodine-131 (131I) exposure after Chernobyl and diffuse goiter risk.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort study (n=10,278, mean thyroid dose 0.63 Gy); logistic regression with linear and linear-exponential dose-response models; screening 10–15 years post-exposure.
- **Key findings**: 131I dose significantly associated with diffuse goiter (EOR/Gy=1.45, 95% CI 0.70–2.41 at <0.5 Gy); iodine deficiency and age at exposure significantly modified dose-response (all p<0.01).
- **Strengths**: Large cohort with individual thyroid dosimetry, standardised ultrasound/palpation screening protocol, and long follow-up.
- **Limitations**: Cross-sectional screening design; residual confounding from unmeasured iodine deficiency markers.
- **Conclusion**: First systematic evaluation shows significant radiation-associated diffuse goiter risk after environmental 131I, with effect modification by iodine deficiency and age.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-1949 / PMID:42301737

## 3. Estimating and discovering heterogeneous treatment effects using machine learning in epidemiological studies: a practical guide — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Int J Epidemiol
- **Pub date**: 2026-04-17
- **Authors**: Komura T et al.
- **Aims**: Provide epidemiologists with a practical, code-accompanied guide for ML-based heterogeneous treatment effect (HTE) estimation.
- **Method**: Methodological review covering meta-learner frameworks and causal forests for conditional average treatment effect (CATE) estimation; applied to a US national sample of older adults.
- **Key findings**: Causal forests and meta-learners reliably identify HTE patterns; calibration diagnostics and scale of effect-measure modification are critical for valid CATE interpretation.
- **Strengths**: Statistical codes released alongside the guide; applicable to both RCTs and observational studies.
- **Limitations**: CATE precision degrades with small samples or extreme high-dimensional confounding; granular CATE can overfit.
- **Conclusion**: Equips epidemiologists with conceptual and practical ML tools for HTE discovery without requiring specialist data-science training.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/ije/dyag092 / PMID:42287695

## 5. Adjuvant radiotherapy and skin cancer risk in breast cancer survivors: a nationwide cohort study in Korea — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Br J Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Chin JH et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether adjuvant radiotherapy increases subsequent skin cancer risk in South Korean breast cancer survivors.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort study (HIRA national registry, n=37,957); 1:1 propensity-score matching (9,928/group); multivariable Cox proportional hazards regression.
- **Key findings**: Adjuvant RT not associated with skin cancer risk (p=0.604); mole presence (HR 5.254), pre-malignant lesions (HR 12.905), and lymphedema (HR 1.978) were significant predictors.
- **Strengths**: Large nationwide registry, propensity-score matching for confounding, first sizeable Asian study on this question.
- **Limitations**: Administrative claims data lack radiation dose and field detail; residual confounding from unmeasured dermatological history possible.
- **Conclusion**: Dermatological risk factors and lymphedema, not radiotherapy, are the primary drivers of skin cancer in Korean breast cancer survivors.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03485-z / PMID:42286233
