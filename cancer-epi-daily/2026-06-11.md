## 1. Post-adjuvant chemotherapy in ctDNA-positive patients with resected colorectal cancer: a randomized phase 3 trial — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-08
- **Authors**: Bando H et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether early FTD/TPI treatment upon ctDNA-detected molecular recurrence improves disease-free survival in resected CRC.
- **Method**: Randomized double-blind phase 3 trial (ALTAIR/CIRCULATE-Japan); 243 ctDNA-positive, radiologically disease-free patients post-adjuvant therapy randomized to FTD/TPI vs placebo.
- **Key findings**: Median DFS 9.30 months (FTD/TPI) vs 5.55 months (placebo); HR 0.79 (95% CI 0.60–1.05), P=0.107 — primary endpoint not met.
- **Strengths**: First RCT to prospectively test ctDNA-triggered treatment strategy; rigorous double-blind phase 3 design.
- **Limitations**: Underpowered at 243 patients; predominantly East Asian cohort limits generalizability; single-agent FTD/TPI may not be optimal.
- **Conclusion**: Post-adjuvant FTD/TPI did not significantly improve DFS in ctDNA-positive CRC, indicating the ctDNA-triggered treatment paradigm needs further refinement.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04428-0 / PMID:42260101

## 2. Blood-based DNA methylation marker model for short-term and long-term lung cancer risk prediction — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: BMC Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-06
- **Authors**: Bhardwaj M et al.
- **Aims**: Develop and externally validate a blood-based DNA methylation model predicting future lung cancer in ever and never smokers.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort study; model trained in ESTHER (n=2,459 Germans); externally validated in HUNT2/HUNT3 (n=233 Norwegians); EWAS-derived CpGs; AUC reported.
- **Key findings**: Derivation AUC 0.84 (95% CI 0.80–0.87); external validation AUC 0.85 in both HUNT2 and HUNT3; stable prediction up to 18 years before diagnosis.
- **Strengths**: External validation in two independent European cohorts; includes never-smokers; long pre-diagnostic lead time demonstrated.
- **Limitations**: Small external validation sample (n=233); not benchmarked against LDCT; clinical translation requires larger prospective studies.
- **Conclusion**: Blood-based DNA methylation model achieves AUC ~0.85 consistently across short and long horizons in independent cohorts, supporting further clinical validation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12916-026-04973-y / PMID:42251373

## 3. Real-world outcomes of immunotherapy in advanced NSCLC patients with comorbidities — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: British Journal of Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-08
- **Authors**: Hektoen HH et al.
- **Aims**: Assess how comorbidities affect treatment receipt and survival outcomes in advanced NSCLC in the immune checkpoint inhibitor era.
- **Method**: Nationwide Norwegian registry study; 9,178 stage IIIB–IV NSCLC patients; multivariable Cox models; pre- vs post-ICI introduction comparison.
- **Key findings**: 2-year OS improved up to threefold post-ICI across all comorbidity groups; CVD/T2D/RMD patients had higher mortality with ICI+chemotherapy vs ICI alone (HRs 1.36, 1.33, 1.38).
- **Strengths**: Large nationwide registry with comprehensive comorbidity and treatment data; pre/post-ICI era design isolates the real-world immunotherapy effect.
- **Limitations**: Residual confounding from unmeasured factors; registry data lack granular molecular/biomarker data.
- **Conclusion**: ICI improved survival across all comorbidity groups, but differential toxicity with ICI+chemotherapy in CVD/T2D/RMD patients supports individualised treatment strategies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03491-1 / PMID:42259946

## 4. Relative Incidence of Multiple Primary Cancers in Male World Trade Center Responders: A Comparison With SEER — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-08
- **Authors**: Turner WM et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether male WTC responders have elevated rates of multiple primary cancers (MPCs) compared with the U.S. general population.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort; 4,815 male WTC responders (2002–2022) vs 3,376,402 SEER cancer patients; age-standardized proportion ratios (PRs).
- **Key findings**: WTC responders had 84% higher MPC proportion (PR=1.84, 95% CI 1.61–2.11); strongest at ages 20–44 (PR=3.76, 95% CI 3.04–4.67).
- **Strengths**: Long 20-year follow-up; large SEER comparator; age-stratified analysis reveals disproportionate young-adult burden.
- **Limitations**: Male-only cohort; potential surveillance bias between WTC and SEER populations; residual confounding from unmeasured carcinogen exposures.
- **Conclusion**: WTC responders bear substantially elevated MPC burden, particularly at younger ages, supporting enhanced long-term cancer monitoring protocols.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0165 / PMID:42257574

## 5. Pancreatic Cancer Risk Stratification Across Diabetes Stages: Development and Internal Validation of a Machine Learning Model — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-08
- **Authors**: Khan S et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a machine learning model for pancreatic cancer risk stratification across the full diabetes spectrum, handling missing clinical data.
- **Method**: Retrospective TriNetX EHR cohort (3,213,551 patients with HbA1c ≥6.5%); XGBoost with 65 variables at 90-day intervals; benchmarked against ENDPAC, Boursi, Cheung models.
- **Key findings**: XGBoost AUROC 0.78 (95% CI 0.77–0.79); 100% patient coverage vs <10% for existing models; outperformed ENDPAC (AUROC 0.79 vs 0.63, P<0.001) on matched subsets.
- **Strengths**: Largest EHR-based pancreatic cancer prediction cohort; handles missing data; covers both new-onset and prevalent diabetes.
- **Limitations**: Internal validation only; no external validation performed; TriNetX EHR data quality varies; requires prospective validation before clinical use.
- **Conclusion**: XGBoost achieves broad pancreatic cancer risk stratification across diabetes subtypes with near-universal patient coverage, pending external validation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-1782 / PMID:42257579

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest highlights two converging themes: ctDNA-guided oncology decision-making is maturing from observational promise to rigorous RCT testing (ALTAIR), with the negative result itself being methodologically instructive. Concurrently, blood-based and EHR-derived predictive models (DNA methylation for lung cancer, XGBoost for pancreatic cancer) continue to advance risk stratification beyond classical clinical variables, though external validation remains the key bottleneck for clinical translation. Real-world registry studies, exemplified by the Norwegian NSCLC comorbidity analysis, are increasingly producing actionable epi evidence that clinical trials systematically exclude.
