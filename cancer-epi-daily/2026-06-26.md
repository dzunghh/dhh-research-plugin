## 1. Metabolic determinants of cancer immunotherapy outcomes identified by plasma profiling — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Suissa D et al.
- **Aims**: Identify plasma metabolites predicting immune-checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) outcomes across multiple cancer types.
- **Method**: Targeted metabolomics + metagenomics of 4,336 plasma samples from 1,714 patients across 5 tumor types and 16 cohorts; multi-modal machine-learning integrating 154 metabolites with clinical variables.
- **Key findings**: Five metabolites plus age, BMI, and renal function predicted 12-month PFS (AUC 0.88 training, 0.73 validation); histidine was favorable, long-chain fatty acids and succinate were negative predictors.
- **Strengths**: Large multi-cohort multi-tumor design externally validated across 7 independent cohorts spanning Europe and North America.
- **Limitations**: Metabolomic panel limited to 154 metabolites; confounding by prior treatments not fully excluded; not all cohorts had longitudinal sampling.
- **Conclusion**: Plasma metabolomics integrated with clinical variables generalizes as a predictive biomarker for ICI response, with histidine supplementation warranting prospective testing.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04481-9 / PMID:42350644

## 2. Deconvolving SARS-CoV-2 mRNA vaccine impact on immunotherapy-related survival — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Discovery
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-22
- **Authors**: Jee J et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether SARS-CoV-2 mRNA vaccination genuinely improves survival in ICI-treated cancer patients (peri-ICI vaccination hypothesis).
- **Method**: Real-world analysis of two US tertiary cancer-center cohorts; temporal stratification by vaccine-availability periods; PFS comparison across pre-vaccination and high-uptake eras.
- **Key findings**: Longer survival with peri-ICI vaccination was observed only in early pandemic (low vaccine availability); no advantage when vaccines were broadly available; PFS in high-uptake periods did not exceed pre-vaccination era.
- **Strengths**: Rigorous temporal-variation approach as natural experiment; independent replication of prior analytic framework in two separate cohorts.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective real-world data limited to two US cancer centers; residual confounding by prognosis possible.
- **Conclusion**: Previously reported vaccination survival advantage is largely explained by selection bias favoring prognostically favorable patients during early vaccine rollout.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/2159-8290.CD-26-0403 / PMID:42330421

## 3. Adherence to the Mediterranean diet and risk of pancreatic cancer: an analysis of 2.3 million participants (DCPP) — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: European Journal of Epidemiology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Shen Q et al.
- **Aims**: Investigate association between Mediterranean diet adherence and pancreatic cancer risk in the largest pooled prospective analysis to date.
- **Method**: Pooled analysis of 23 prospective cohorts (DCPP), 2,315,406 individuals, 10,748 pancreatic cancer cases; Cox proportional hazards models + random-effects meta-analysis; aMED and maMED diet scores.
- **Key findings**: No significant association between Mediterranean diet and pancreatic cancer risk (pooled HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.90–1.02 for quartile 4 vs 1); maMED positively associated with risk in past smokers only (HR 1.04, 95% CI 1.00–1.09).
- **Strengths**: Largest pooled analysis addressing this question; standardized methods across 23 cohorts in multiple countries.
- **Limitations**: Dietary assessment methods vary across cohorts; possible underreporting; observational design precludes causal inference.
- **Conclusion**: Mediterranean diet adherence is not associated with pancreatic cancer risk in this definitive international analysis, resolving prior inconsistency with well-powered null evidence.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1007/s10654-026-01418-x / PMID:42348090

## 4. Neoadjuvant stereotactic body radiation therapy with durvalumab and oleclumab in ER⁺HER2⁻ breast cancer: Neo-CheckRay randomized phase 2 trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nature Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: De Caluwe A et al.
- **Aims**: Test whether immune-modulating SBRT (iSBRT) with anti-PD-L1 ± anti-CD73 improves pathologic complete response in PD-L1-negative ER⁺HER2⁻ early breast cancer.
- **Method**: Phase 2, three-arm, randomized multicenter trial (NCT03875573); 147 patients; neoadjuvant chemotherapy ± iSBRT ± durvalumab ± oleclumab; primary endpoint residual cancer burden 0/1 rate.
- **Key findings**: pCR rates 16.7% (no ICI), 29.4% (single ICI), 33.3% (double ICI); statistically significant in MammaPrint High Risk subgroup (P=0.040); PD-L1-negative pCR 3.4% vs 28.1% vs 30.0%.
- **Strengths**: Randomized multicenter design with paired biopsies for biomarker analysis; meaningful signal in immune-cold PD-L1-negative subgroup.
- **Limitations**: Phase 2 with 147 patients only; primary endpoint did not reach significance in ITT population; requires confirmatory Phase 3.
- **Conclusion**: iSBRT + anti-PD-L1 converts immune-cold ER⁺HER2⁻ breast tumors to inflamed phenotype, improving pCR particularly in PD-L1-negative disease.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04453-z / PMID:42350643

## 5. Circulating tumor-associated autoantibody signatures for diagnosis and prognosis in small-cell lung cancer and lung adenocarcinoma — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: British Journal of Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Liu C et al.
- **Aims**: Identify serum tumor-associated autoantibody (TAAb) panels for early detection and prognosis in SCLC and lung adenocarcinoma.
- **Method**: Protein-array screening and two-stage ELISA validation in 695 serum samples; diagnostic models compared with ProGRP and CEA; prediagnostic sera tested up to 2 years before diagnosis.
- **Key findings**: Six TAAbs (SCLC) and four (LUAD) achieved AUC >0.8, outperforming conventional markers; TAAbs detectable in prediagnostic sera up to 2 years before clinical diagnosis.
- **Strengths**: Two-stage validation design; novel mechanistic link to HLA class II amino-acid polymorphisms; prediagnostic lead time up to 2 years demonstrated.
- **Limitations**: Moderate sample size (n=695); single-institution design; requires external multicohort validation for clinical utility.
- **Conclusion**: Distinct TAAb panels enable accurate early detection and prognostication for SCLC and LUAD, with a clinically meaningful 2-year pre-diagnostic window.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03504-z / PMID:42350789

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest reflects two dominant themes: methodological rigour in real-world oncology evidence and the scaling of multi-cohort epidemiology. The vaccine-ICI survival paper (Jee et al.) is notable for explicitly deploying temporal-variation analysis to debunk a prior selection-bias artifact — a model for causal inference in cancer pharmacoepidemiology. The Mediterranean diet pooled analysis (Shen et al.) closes a long-standing question by providing definitive null evidence from 2.3 million participants, underscoring the value of the DCPP infrastructure. The plasma metabolomics study (Suissa et al.) bridges epidemiology and precision oncology by demonstrating multi-cohort generalizability of metabolomic ICI-response predictors — the type of translational epi methodology increasingly appearing in high-impact journals.
