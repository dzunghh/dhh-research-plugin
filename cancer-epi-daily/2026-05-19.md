- **Title:** Global Skin Cancer Burden From 1990 to 2023 and Projection to 2050
- **Authors:** Zhou Y et al. (Youyou Zhou, Shenzhen People's Hospital)
- **Journal:** JAMA Dermatology
- **EDAT:** 2026-05-13
- **Score:** 6/6 (rigour 2 + novelty 2 + reproducibility 2)
- **Aims:** Quantify global burden of melanoma, cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC), and basal cell carcinoma (BCC) from 1990 to 2023, and project trends to 2050.
- **Method:** Cross-sectional analysis of GBD 2023 data across 204 countries; Bayesian age-period-cohort (BAPC) model for 2050 projections; stratified by sex, age, and Socio-demographic Index (SDI).
- **Key findings:** Melanoma DALYs projected to exceed 3.3 M by 2050; cSCC 4.0 M; BCC 5.0 M. East Asia melanoma burden projected +258.8%. Steepest absolute and relative rises in low- and middle-SDI regions. Prevention and early-detection capacity lags most where burden is growing fastest.
- **Strengths:** GBD 2023 (most recent global dataset); simultaneous three-cancer analysis; 204-country coverage; BAPC projection methodology.
- **Limitations:** GBD ecological design; variable data quality by country; UVR exposure proxies are approximate.
- **Conclusion:** Global skin cancer burden rising sharply, especially in low-SDI settings; prevention programmes must scale proportionately.
- **ID:** DOI:10.1001/jamadermatol.2026.0964

- **Title:** Rural-Urban Disparities in Hepatocellular Carcinoma Incidence and Mortality in the United States, 2001–2022
- **Authors:** Damgacioglu H, Uygun G, Deshmukh AA, Kaseb AO, Noonan AM, Demir T
- **Journal:** JAMA Network Open
- **EDAT:** 2026-05-13
- **Score:** 6/6 (rigour 2 + novelty 2 + reproducibility 2)
- **Aims:** Characterise trends in HCC incidence and incidence-based mortality by urban-rural classification in the US.
- **Method:** Population-based cohort using SEER database, 2001–2022; joinpoint regression for trend analysis; stratified by sex, race/ethnicity, and stage at diagnosis.
- **Key findings:** HCC incidence rose in rural counties while declining in urban counties over the study period. Incidence-based mortality rose or remained stable in rural areas but declined in urban settings. Etiologic shift in rural HCC toward metabolic-associated steatohepatitis, obesity, and alcohol-related liver disease.
- **Strengths:** SEER national cancer registry; 22-year observation window; comprehensive stratification by multiple sociodemographic variables.
- **Limitations:** SEER may undercount rural cases; no individual-level etiology data; rural heterogeneity not fully captured by urban-rural classification codes.
- **Conclusion:** Diverging rural-urban HCC trajectories driven by shifting etiology demand targeted metabolic intervention and surveillance in rural populations.
- **ID:** DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.12323

- **Title:** Survival and Recurrence Outcomes Associated With GLP-1 Receptor Agonist Use in Patients With Breast Cancer and Obesity or Diabetes
- **Authors:** Tatum KL, Dahman B, Stevenson A et al. (Kristina L. Tatum, Virginia Commonwealth University)
- **Journal:** JAMA Network Open
- **EDAT:** 2026-05-13
- **Score:** 4/6 (rigour 1 + novelty 2 + reproducibility 1)
- **Aims:** Assess the association between GLP-1 receptor agonist use and all-cause mortality and recurrence in breast cancer patients with obesity or diabetes.
- **Method:** Retrospective cohort; TriNetX US Collaborative Network; 841,831 women with stage I–III breast cancer (2006–2023); propensity-score matching on 30+ variables; up to 10-year follow-up.
- **Key findings:** In obesity group, GLP-1 use associated with HR 0.35 (95% CI 0.21–0.58) for all-cause mortality and HR 0.44 (95% CI 0.30–0.64) for recurrence vs non-users. Similar direction of benefit observed in diabetes group.
- **Strengths:** Largest real-world GLP-1/breast cancer cohort to date; 841,831 patients; multiple pre-specified subgroup analyses; propensity-score matched design.
- **Limitations:** Retrospective TriNetX design; residual confounding by indication likely; healthy adherer effect possible; TriNetX not publicly accessible, limiting replication.
- **Conclusion:** GLP-1 receptor agonist use associated with substantially improved survival and reduced recurrence in breast cancer patients with obesity or diabetes; prospective RCTs urgently needed to confirm causality.
- **ID:** DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.12133
