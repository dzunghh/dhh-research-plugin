## 1. Interval From Cancer Diagnosis to Depression Onset and Risk of Dementia in Cancer Survivors — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Lee S et al.
- **Aims**: Examine how timing of post-cancer depression relates to dementia risk comparing time-fixed and time-varying Cox models.
- **Method**: Nationwide Korean NHI cohort (n=385,092 cancer survivors ≥55 years; 2009–2013 diagnosis; median 11.7 years follow-up); Cox proportional hazards with time-fixed and time-varying depression exposures.
- **Key findings**: 18.6% developed all-cause dementia; time-varying models showed consistently elevated risk (aHR 2.09, 95% CI 2.02–2.17 for depression >5 years post-cancer); time-fixed models showed spurious inverse association, exposing immortal time bias.
- **Strengths**: Very large national cohort with exceptionally long follow-up; explicit methodological comparison of modeling approaches.
- **Limitations**: ICD-10 code-based outcomes may miss mild depression or early dementia; no data on depression treatment type.
- **Conclusion**: Post-cancer depression at any time point is associated with elevated dementia risk; time-fixed analyses introduce immortal time bias and should be avoided in survivorship research.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0336 / PMID:42206989

## 2. Longitudinal Risk for Suicidal Self-Directed Violence Among Veterans With Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Sullivan DR et al.
- **Aims**: Assess longitudinal rates and risk factors for fatal and nonfatal suicidal self-directed violence (SSDV) among veterans with cancer.
- **Method**: National cohort of 292,271 veterans with invasive cancer (2014–2023); VA oncology and suicide registries; multivariable Cox models estimating adjusted hazard ratios.
- **Key findings**: 2,400 SSDV events (rate 203/100,000 person-years); CNS, pancreas, H&N cancers had highest rates; risk remained elevated >5 years post-diagnosis for younger and unmarried veterans.
- **Strengths**: Largest veteran cancer cohort systematically tracking both fatal and nonfatal suicide attempts; 10-year longitudinal follow-up.
- **Limitations**: VA population (predominantly older White males) limits generalizability to community cancer patients.
- **Conclusion**: SSDV risk persists years into survivorship and varies by cancer type and patient demographics, necessitating sustained, tailored screening beyond the diagnosis period.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1459 / PMID:42207508

## 3. Addition of Intravesical Recombinant BCG to Perioperative Chemo-Immunotherapy in Muscle-Invasive Bladder Cancer: Primary Analysis of SAKK 06/19 — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-29
- **Authors**: Cathomas R et al.
- **Aims**: Investigate neoadjuvant intravesical recombinant BCG (rBCG, VPM1002BC) combined with atezolizumab plus cisplatin/gemcitabine prior to radical cystectomy in MIBC.
- **Method**: Single-arm Phase II trial (SAKK 06/19; cT2-T4a N0-1 MIBC; n=47); Simon's minimax 2-stage design; primary endpoint centrally reviewed pathological complete response (pCR ypT0 ypN0).
- **Key findings**: pCR 68% (27/40; one-sided 95% CI lower boundary 53%); pathological overall response 83% (33/40); grade 3+ AEs: chemotherapy 38%, atezolizumab 15%, rBCG 9%.
- **Strengths**: First trial combining intravesical rBCG with systemic chemo-immunotherapy in MIBC; centrally reviewed histopathological outcomes.
- **Limitations**: Single-arm, non-randomised, small n=47; results require confirmation in a randomised controlled trial.
- **Conclusion**: High pCR and pathological response rates support further investigation of intravesical rBCG plus chemo-immunotherapy in a prospective randomised MIBC trial.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-26-00845 / PMID:42212670

## 4. Trends and Disparities in Disability Among US Adult Cancer Survivors, 2016-2024 — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Pu J et al.
- **Aims**: Estimate 8-year disability prevalence trends and demographic disparities among US adult cancer survivors.
- **Method**: Nationally representative BRFSS serial cross-sectional data (2016–2024; n=311,143 cancer survivors); survey-weighted multivariable logistic regression across six disability domains.
- **Key findings**: Disability prevalence rose 5.67 percentage points (40.12% to 45.78%); steepest increase 2020–2021 (+4.04%); mobility disability most prevalent (28.73% in 2024); largest increases in 18–34-year-olds and males.
- **Strengths**: Very large nationally representative sample; 8-year trend captures COVID-era changes in disability burden.
- **Limitations**: Self-reported cancer history and disability; cross-sectional annual design precludes causal inference.
- **Conclusion**: Disability is rising and unevenly distributed among cancer survivors, underscoring the need for proactive rehabilitative surveillance in oncology care.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0096 / PMID:42206987

## 5. Low Muscle Density Is Associated With Morbidity and Systemic Inflammation in Colorectal Cancer, Independent of Tumor Factors — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Aro R et al.
- **Aims**: Identify sex- and BMI-specific thresholds for skeletal muscle density (SMD) and index (SMI) and their prognostic value in colorectal cancer.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort of 927 stage I–IV CRC patients; preoperative CT-based body composition; optimal stratification; Kaplan-Meier and multivariable Cox regression.
- **Key findings**: Low SMD (not SMI) independently predicted worse cancer-specific survival (HR 4.29, 95% CI 1.86–9.91) and OS (HR 3.42, 95% CI 1.62–7.25) in women; associated with elevated CRP and IL-6.
- **Strengths**: Distinguishes SMD from SMI; explicitly links muscle quality to systemic inflammation independently of tumor characteristics.
- **Limitations**: Reproducible cutoff values for SMD could not be established; single-centre retrospective design limits generalisability.
- **Conclusion**: SMD is a sex-specific prognostic marker in CRC; incorporating SMD into preoperative risk stratification may improve individualised management for female patients.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0189 / PMID:42206984

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest is dominated by cancer survivorship epidemiology, with three papers examining long-term consequences of cancer diagnosis — dementia risk, suicidal behaviour, and disability — reflecting a field-wide shift toward patient-centred outcomes research. The standout methodological contribution is the immortal time bias demonstration (PMID:42206989), which provides a textbook illustration of how exposure timing misclassification in time-fixed Cox models can invert the estimated direction of an association in survivorship studies. The SAKK 06/19 trial represents an important proof-of-concept for intravesical immunotherapy in muscle-invasive disease, though single-arm Phase II data must be interpreted cautiously pending randomised confirmation.
