## 1. Co-occurring clonal hematopoiesis exhibits strong selection and high leukemia risk — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Barnao KM et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise prevalence, selection pressure, and malignancy risk of co-occurring clonal hematopoiesis (≥2 CH mutations) in cancer-free and cancer-affected populations.
- **Method**: Population genomics analysis in 546,090 multi-ethnic biobank participants; co-occurrence of CHIP and mosaic chromosomal alterations (mCAs) modelled for selection and disease risk.
- **Key findings**: Co-occurring CH present in 1.6% of cancer-free individuals with up to 804× enrichment vs. independent expectations. HRs for myeloid/lymphoid malignancies exceeded 40 when CHIP and mCAs overlapped genomically.
- **Strengths**: Largest CH co-occurrence study to date; multi-cohort biobank design with strong statistical power.
- **Limitations**: Observational; treatment-induced selection in prior-cancer subgroup (3.6%) may inflate risk estimates.
- **Conclusion**: Co-occurring CH is evolutionarily selected in aging and identifies clones conferring clinically significant leukemia risk.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73302-x / PMID:42168198

## 2. Beyond sex determination: the Y chromosome in male cancers — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Reviews Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Abdel-Hafiz HA et al.
- **Aims**: Synthesise evidence on loss of the Y chromosome (LOY) as a somatic alteration driving cancer susceptibility, progression, and immune escape in males.
- **Method**: Narrative review integrating CRISPR-induced LOY models, single-cell/bulk sequencing, bioinformatic LOY-scoring algorithms, and population cohort studies.
- **Key findings**: LOY is the most prevalent somatic genomic alteration in males; linked to haematological malignancy susceptibility and context-dependent prognostic impact in solid tumours, with effects on immune surveillance and tumour microenvironment.
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive synthesis spanning molecular mechanisms, population data, and precision-oncology implications; proposes LOY as a non-invasive blood/urine biomarker.
- **Limitations**: Review design; causal vs. passenger role of LOY remains unresolved across tumour types.
- **Conclusion**: LOY warrants clinical integration as a cancer-risk biomarker and immunotherapy-response predictor in males.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41568-026-00935-x / PMID:42168613

## 3. Endocytic evasion confers resistance to antibody-drug conjugates therapy in cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Cell
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Wang Y et al.
- **Aims**: Identify the mechanism by which urothelial carcinoma cells develop resistance to NECTIN4-targeting ADC (enfortumab vedotin) despite maintaining target expression.
- **Method**: Paired spatial transcriptomics and single-cell RNA-seq of pre/post-ADC patient tumours, humanised mouse models, and resistant cell-line systems; pharmacologic rescue experiments.
- **Key findings**: AKR1C1 binds NECTIN4 and blocks AP2M1-dependent clathrin-mediated ADC internalisation; AKR1C1-WWP2 axis exports payload via extracellular vesicles. AKR1C1 inhibition restored ADC sensitivity in preclinical models.
- **Strengths**: Multimodal genomics with paired patient samples and orthogonal mechanistic validation; druggable target identified.
- **Limitations**: Clinical validation of AKR1C1 inhibition strategy not yet performed; preclinical models may not fully recapitulate tumour heterogeneity.
- **Conclusion**: AKR1C1-mediated endocytic evasion is a druggable resistance mechanism broadly relevant to ADC-treated cancers.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.ccell.2026.04.010 / PMID:42167230

## 4. Distinct genomic and immunologic tumor evolution in germline TP53-driven breast cancers — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Boruah N et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise the genomic and immune microenvironment landscape of Li-Fraumeni Syndrome (LFS) breast cancer compared with sporadic premenopausal breast cancer.
- **Method**: Multimodal analysis (whole-genome sequencing, transcriptomics, spatial immune profiling) of LFS-BC versus sporadic BC; in situ-to-invasive progression modelled within LFS cohort.
- **Key findings**: Nearly all LFS-BCs show biallelic TP53 loss with high aneuploid short amplifications and exclusive ERBB2 amplification; LFS-BC has lower CD8+ but higher proliferating cytotoxic T-cells, with chromosomal instability rising and T-cells declining at invasion.
- **Strengths**: First multimodal comparison of LFS versus sporadic premenopausal BC with spatial immune resolution.
- **Limitations**: Small LFS cohort (rare syndrome); prospective validation and larger series needed.
- **Conclusion**: LFS-BC has distinct genomic instability and immune suppression features from sporadic BC, informing tailored therapeutic strategies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73163-4 / PMID:42168203

## 5. Datopotamab deruxtecan plus durvalumab as first-line treatment for metastatic triple-negative breast cancer: BEGONIA Arms 7 and 8 — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-20
- **Authors**: Schmid P et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety and efficacy of TROP2-ADC datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd) plus anti-PD-L1 durvalumab as first-line treatment for unresectable/metastatic TNBC.
- **Method**: Phase Ib/II open-label Simon's 2-stage platform trial (BEGONIA); Arm 7 (PD-L1 unselected, n=62), Arm 8 (PD-L1-high, n=33); primary endpoints: safety and confirmed ORR.
- **Key findings**: Arm 7 cORR 79.0% (95% CI 66.8–88.3), median DoR 17.6 months, median PFS 14.0 months; Arm 8 cORR 81.8% (95% CI 64.5–93.0). No new safety signals.
- **Strengths**: High ORR across PD-L1-unselected population; mature Arm 7 follow-up (median 35 months) with durable responses.
- **Limitations**: Non-randomised single-arm design; Arm 8 DoR/PFS immature at data cut-off (median follow-up 10.7 months).
- **Conclusion**: Dato-DXd plus durvalumab shows substantial, durable first-line activity in metastatic TNBC regardless of PD-L1 status.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.693 / PMID:42167437
