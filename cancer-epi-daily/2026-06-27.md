## 1. Serum CA19.9 for Detecting High-Grade Dysplasia and Invasive Carcinoma in Patients With Intraductal Papillary Mucinous Neoplasm: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Network Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Leseman CA et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate diagnostic accuracy of serum CA19.9 (≥37 U/mL) for high-grade dysplasia or invasive carcinoma in IPMN patients per current guideline use.
- **Method**: Systematic review and random-effects meta-analysis of 24 studies (n=5,281 resected IPMN patients); QUADAS-2 bias assessment; GRADE evidence certainty; SROC curves with AUC.
- **Key findings**: Sensitivity 0.35 (95% CI 0.29–0.43), specificity 0.90 (0.87–0.92); AUC 0.78; NLR 0.72 — poor for ruling out HGD/IC.
- **Strengths**: Large pooled sample across 24 studies; pre-registered Cochrane protocol; rigorous QUADAS-2 and GRADE appraisal.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective surgical cohorts introduce selection bias; heterogeneity in IPMN classification criteria across contributing studies.
- **Conclusion**: CA19.9 at current cutoff should not be used as a standalone or screening test; limited rule-in value may support surgical decision-making at moderate pretest probability.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.17587 / PMID:42360746

## 2. Inhibition of ADSS2-mediated de novo AMP biosynthesis re-sensitizes acute myeloid leukemia to BH3 mimetics — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-26
- **Authors**: He X et al.
- **Aims**: Identify metabolic drivers of BH3 mimetic (venetoclax/MCL1i) resistance in AML and develop pharmacological countermeasures.
- **Method**: Dynamic BH3-priming CRISPR screen in drug-resistant AML; single-cell sequencing of patient-derived xenografts; ADSS2 antagonist synthesis and preclinical combination testing.
- **Key findings**: ADSS2 deletion re-sensitizes resistant AML; high ADSS2 activity in TP53-mutant cells correlates with poor venetoclax response; AMPK-driven mitophagy is the mechanistic link.
- **Strengths**: Multi-modal validation (CRISPR screen, PDX, scRNA-seq, novel antagonist); direct mechanistic-to-pharmacological pipeline.
- **Limitations**: Entirely preclinical; clinical translation requires prospective in-human studies.
- **Conclusion**: AMP synthesis via ADSS2 drives BH3 mimetic resistance; combined ADSS2 inhibition and BH3 mimetics is a promising therapeutic strategy for drug-resistant AML.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s43018-026-01184-5 / PMID:42362999
