## 1. Adjuvant Nivolumab vs Observation in Resected Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer: A Randomized Clinical Trial — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Chaft JE et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether adjuvant nivolumab improves disease-free survival after resection of EGFR/ALK-wild-type NSCLC.
- **Method**: Open-label phase 3 RCT (N=935) at 378 US centres; nivolumab 480 mg IV q4w×1 yr vs observation; co-primary DFS in ITT and PD-L1≥50%; median follow-up 72.6 months.
- **Key findings**: Median DFS 71.3 vs 68.8 months (HR 0.97); trial stopped for futility. PD-L1≥50% subset showed no benefit (HR 0.86).
- **Strengths**: Large multicentre RCT with nearly 6-year median follow-up and pre-specified co-primary endpoints.
- **Limitations**: Open-label design; early closure at 75% information reduces power.
- **Conclusion**: Adjuvant nivolumab after planned chemo/RT does not improve DFS in resected EGFR/ALK-wild-type NSCLC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jama.2026.8992 / PMID:42224490

## 2. Pemigatinib for Unresectable or Metastatic Cholangiocarcinoma With FGFR2 Rearrangement: Results From the Phase 3 FIGHT-302 Trial — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Bekaii-Saab TS et al.
- **Aims**: Assess first-line pemigatinib vs gemcitabine-cisplatin in FGFR2-rearranged advanced cholangiocarcinoma.
- **Method**: Phase 3 RCT (NCT03656536); 1:1 randomisation; pemigatinib 13.5 mg/day vs chemotherapy; primary endpoint PFS; N=167 before early closure.
- **Key findings**: Median PFS 8.3 vs 6.8 months (HR 0.58, 95%CI 0.39–0.87); ORR 47% vs 15%; median OS similar (24.4 vs 25.0 months).
- **Strengths**: Largest first-line RCT of targeted therapy in FGFR2-selected cholangiocarcinoma; crossover permitted.
- **Limitations**: Underpowered due to early closure; no OS benefit demonstrated.
- **Conclusion**: First-line pemigatinib significantly improved PFS over chemotherapy in FGFR2-rearranged cholangiocarcinoma.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-26-00788 / PMID:42223137

## 3. EP4 Antagonist ONO-4578 Plus Nivolumab and Chemotherapy in HER2-Negative Unresectable Advanced or Recurrent Gastric or Gastroesophageal Junction Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Nakayama I et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether EP4 antagonist ONO-4578 enhances nivolumab plus chemotherapy in first-line HER2-negative gastric/GEJ cancer.
- **Method**: Double-blind phase 2 RCT; 2:1 randomisation (ONO-4578 n=150 vs placebo n=76); nivolumab + oxaliplatin-based chemo backbone; primary endpoint investigator-assessed PFS.
- **Key findings**: ONO-4578 significantly improved PFS (HR 0.67, 90%CI 0.48–0.92); ORR 62.0% vs 48.7%; OS HR 0.60 at prespecified limited follow-up.
- **Strengths**: Double-blind RCT targeting novel EP4/PGE2 immunosuppressive axis; encouraging OS signal.
- **Limitations**: Phase 2; 90% CI used for PFS; no clear benefit in PD-L1 CPS<1 patients.
- **Conclusion**: ONO-4578 regimen demonstrated promising first-line efficacy warranting phase 3 confirmation in gastric/GEJ cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-26-01072 / PMID:42223130
