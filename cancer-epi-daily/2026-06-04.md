## 1. International disruptions to cancer diagnosis during the COVID-19 pandemic — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: Morgan E et al.
- **Aims**: Quantify international disruptions to cancer incidence and stage at diagnosis during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020.
- **Method**: Population-based registry study of 2.6 million patients across 18 jurisdictions in 7 countries (ICBP); observed vs predicted incidence rates 2015–2020.
- **Key findings**: 55,713 (16%) of expected cases were missing April–December 2020; largest deficits for prostate (24%), breast (18%), melanoma (18%). UK prostate deficit reached 54% in April–July 2020.
- **Strengths**: Largest international multi-cancer COVID registry analysis; harmonised ICBP methodology across high-income countries.
- **Limitations**: Cannot confirm whether missed diagnoses were later diagnosed or presented at advanced stage.
- **Conclusion**: COVID lockdowns caused substantial, cancer-type-specific diagnosis gaps in screen-detectable cancers with implications for future stage-shift and mortality.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00089-6 / PMID:42225103

## 2. HPV16 genetic variation and positive natural selection driven by HLA class I — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nat Commun
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-02
- **Authors**: Nelson CW et al.
- **Aims**: Identify HPV16 codons under positive natural selection and test whether HLA class I immune pressure drives viral evolution toward cancer persistence.
- **Method**: Evolutionary genomic analysis of 4,704 HPV16 whole genomes; dN/dS >1, convergence, and HLA epitope-overlap tests; case-control comparison in cervical precancer/cancer.
- **Key findings**: 56 codons under positive selection overlap CTL epitopes restricted by HLA-B*07:02 (OR=4.9; 95%CI 2.1–10.3); sub/lineage divergence overlap OR=19.1 (95%CI 10.5–34.7).
- **Strengths**: Largest HPV16 whole-genome dataset; multiple converging evolutionary lines of evidence from NCI Division of Cancer Epidemiology.
- **Limitations**: Population associations cannot confirm causal HLA-mediated escape at the individual patient level.
- **Conclusion**: HPV16 immune escape via HLA-restricted CTL pressure may explain why similar viral isolates produce disparate infection outcomes and cancer risk.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73531-0 / PMID:42230578

## 3. Biologically informed instrument selection for dietary Mendelian randomization using chemosensory genetics — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: BMC Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Hwang LD et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a biologically-informed framework for selecting valid genetic instruments for dietary exposures in Mendelian randomization to reduce pleiotropy.
- **Method**: Multi-stage filtering of 1,214 nonsynonymous variants in 30 taste and 295 olfactory receptor genes; validated in UK Biobank and replicated in ALSPAC; MR application on cardiometabolic outcomes.
- **Key findings**: 24 candidate instruments identified for 20 foods; onion-liking instrument met all high-confidence criteria; SES and food-specificity filters substantially reduced pleiotropic risk.
- **Strengths**: Biologically-principled instrument selection; multi-cohort replication; directly addresses key MR assumption violations in nutritional epidemiology.
- **Limitations**: Instrumental relevance for actual food intake (vs liking) requires further validation; framework demonstrated on few high-confidence instruments.
- **Conclusion**: Chemosensory receptor genetics provide biologically valid, pleiotropically-conservative instruments for dietary MR studies of cancer and cardiometabolic outcomes.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12916-026-04966-x / PMID:42231279

## 4. Predictiveness curve and geometric summaries for evaluating cancer risk prediction models — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Am J Epidemiol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-03
- **Authors**: Chiu WM et al.
- **Aims**: Extend the predictiveness curve with geometric summaries to enable more informative, purpose-specific evaluation of risk prediction models beyond AUC.
- **Method**: Mathematical derivation of Pietra index, Gini index, and scaled Brier score from the predictiveness curve; three-step post-processing (cross-validation, isotonic recalibration, bootstrap averaging); applied to 23,839 lung cancer patients in Taiwan Cancer Registry.
- **Key findings**: In lung cancer, 25.2% were very low risk (<10% fatality) and 50.2% high risk (>75% fatality); Pietra minimises gray zone, Gini maximises separation, scaled Brier increases certainty.
- **Strengths**: Addresses fundamental AUC limitations; population-oriented framework illustrated on a large real-world cancer registry.
- **Limitations**: Geometric summaries are calibration-dependent; may not capture individual-level precision for clinical decision support.
- **Conclusion**: The predictiveness curve and its Gini/Pietra/Brier geometry provide a richer, purpose-specific toolkit for cancer risk model evaluation and reporting.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/aje/kwag123 / PMID:42233710

## 5. Dietary and lifestyle patterns in young lung cancer patients with mutation-specific phenotypes — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-03
- **Authors**: Gorbatov SD et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise dietary and environmental exposures in young lung cancer patients stratified by oncogenic mutation pathway.
- **Method**: Cross-sectional analysis of 187 patients from the Epidemiology of Young Lung Cancer study; Healthy Eating Index (HEI)-2015 compared against US NHANES reference via one-sample t-tests.
- **Key findings**: EGFR and Fusion-Positive patients had higher diet quality (HEI 64.9 and 65.5 vs US reference 58); oral contraceptive use elevated across all groups (81.7–90.2%); 68.8% of Other/Mixed mutation patients never used tobacco.
- **Strengths**: First mutation-pathway-stratified dietary analysis in young lung cancer; identifies novel hormonal and dietary exposures in a tobacco-naïve population.
- **Limitations**: Small sample (n=187), cross-sectional design, potential dietary recall bias; HEI-2015 comparison to general population may not be appropriate.
- **Conclusion**: Mutation-specific dietary and hormonal exposure patterns in young lung cancer point to non-tobacco environmental pathways warranting prospective investigation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0057 / PMID:42233968

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest spans three themes: pandemic-driven disruption to cancer detection, viral cancer etiology, and epi-methodological innovation. The ICBP study provides the clearest international quantification of pandemic-driven cancer diagnosis gaps, underscoring that screen-detectable cancers bore the greatest burden of missed diagnoses. The HPV16-HLA evolutionary paper from NCI exemplifies how large-scale viral genomic epidemiology can dissect cancer etiology at population scale with OR-level precision. Two methodological contributions—a biologically-principled MR instrument framework using chemosensory genetics and a geometric predictiveness-curve toolkit—jointly advance rigor in causal inference and risk stratification, areas central to cancer epidemiology's evolving methodology.
