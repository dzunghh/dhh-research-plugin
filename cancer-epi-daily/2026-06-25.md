## 1. Long-Term Outcomes in Patients With Recurrent Ovarian Cancer and Exceptional Response to PARP Inhibitors — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Haggstrom L et al.
- **Aims**: Determine long-term outcomes in platinum-sensitive recurrent ovarian cancer patients with exceptional response (PFS ≥5 years) to PARP inhibitors.
- **Method**: International multicenter retrospective cohort, 41 sites, 14 countries, n=320, median follow-up 6.8 years.
- **Key findings**: 10-year PFS 78.7%; patients discontinuing PARP inhibitors without progression had 10-year PFS 90.1% vs 72.5% for those continuing. Late MDS/AML occurred in 1.6%.
- **Strengths**: Largest exceptional-responder cohort to date; multinational, long follow-up; genotype-phenotype analysis.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective design with potential selection bias toward academic-center patients.
- **Conclusion**: Functional cure may be achievable in a subset; planned PARP inhibitor discontinuation appears safe in exceptional responders.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1924 / PMID:42348196

## 2. The Mutational Landscape of STING-Induced Immunity — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Zhang B et al.
- **Aims**: Systematically map the sequence-function landscape of STING across thousands of single amino-acid variants.
- **Method**: Massively parallel variant-effect assay profiling STING amino-acid substitutions; cryo-EM structures of hyperactive variants.
- **Key findings**: Identified determinants controlling STING immunostimulatory capacity; single substitutions can decouple interferon induction from non-canonical autophagy. Naturally occurring variants show clinical and evolutionary relevance.
- **Strengths**: Systematic scale; structural validation by cryo-EM; direct relevance to cancer immunotherapy target development.
- **Limitations**: Primarily in vitro/cell-based assay; in vivo immunostimulatory phenotypes not fully characterised.
- **Conclusion**: Defines molecular principles tuning STING activity with implications for cancer immune response and therapeutic design.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41586-026-10685-3 / PMID:42343134

## 3. Alternate RNA Decoding Results in Stable and Abundant Proteins in Mammals — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Tsour S et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise how alternate translation (deviations from the genetic code) contributes to proteome diversity across cancer types and healthy human tissues.
- **Method**: Multi-omics analysis of >1,000 human samples including 6 cancer types and 26 normal tissues; identified 8,746 unique amino-acid substitutions from 60,803 fragmentation spectra.
- **Key findings**: Alternate-translation products exceeded canonical protein abundance for hundreds of proteins; cancer and tissue specificity detected; pattern conserved in mice.
- **Strengths**: Largest cross-cancer proteomic survey of alternate translation; multi-omics design; cross-species validation.
- **Limitations**: Difficult to fully exclude sequencing artefacts; mechanisms of codon recoding incompletely established.
- **Conclusion**: Alternate RNA decoding substantially diversifies mammalian proteomes with cancer-type-specific signatures.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41586-026-10678-2 / PMID:42343131

## 4. Neoadjuvant Paclitaxel, Trastuzumab, and Pertuzumab for Stage II–III ERBB2-Positive Breast Cancer: Secondary Analysis of the DAPHNe Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Tarantino P et al.
- **Aims**: Assess 5-year outcomes and ctDNA dynamics after abbreviated neoadjuvant THP in early ERBB2-positive breast cancer.
- **Method**: Prespecified secondary analysis of single-arm phase 2 DAPHNe trial (n=98); ultrasensitive whole-genome tumor-informed ctDNA assay in 57 patients; median 5.2-year follow-up.
- **Key findings**: 5-year EFS 99%, distant recurrence-free interval 100%; baseline ctDNA detected in 89.5%, cleared after therapy in 96.1%.
- **Strengths**: Prespecified design; ultrasensitive ctDNA platform; long follow-up validating abbreviated neoadjuvant approach.
- **Limitations**: Single-arm non-randomised; small ctDNA substudy (n=57); limited generalisability beyond academic centres.
- **Conclusion**: Neoadjuvant THP produces excellent long-term outcomes; near-universal ctDNA clearance supports investigation of ctDNA-guided de-escalation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.2023 / PMID:42348190
