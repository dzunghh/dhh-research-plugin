## 1. Assessing Racial Disparities in Healthcare Expenditures via Mediator Distribution Shifts — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Statistics in Medicine (Stat Med)
- **Pub date**: 2026-06 (epub ahead of issue)
- **Authors**: Ou X et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a causal mediation framework to decompose racial healthcare-expenditure disparities through shifts in mediator distributions.
- **Method**: Influence-function-based semiparametric estimators with super learners and two-part models applied to MEPS data (2009–2016).
- **Key findings**: Largest gaps were between non-Hispanic Whites and Hispanics; SES and health status explained the greatest share; residual disparities persisted after equalising mediators.
- **Strengths**: Doubly-robust estimation with machine-learning nuisance fitting; large nationally representative MEPS dataset reduces selection bias.
- **Limitations**: Healthcare expenditures not cancer-specific; unmeasured structural confounders likely explain residual gaps.
- **Conclusion**: Novel mediator-distribution-shift decomposition provides actionable levers (SES, insurance access) for reducing health-expenditure inequity.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1002/sim.70606 / PMID:42201641

## 2. Gemcitabine and nab-paclitaxel with or without the VDR agonist paricalcitol for metastatic pancreatic cancer: a randomized, multiarm, run-in phase trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Cancer (Nat Cancer)
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-25
- **Authors**: Perez KJ et al.
- **Aims**: Establish safety of adding paricalcitol (VDR agonist) to gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel and characterise pharmacodynamic stromal effects in metastatic pancreatic cancer.
- **Method**: Randomised 3-arm run-in trial (N=36); pre- and on-treatment biopsies assessed αSMA+ fibroblast density, VDR activation, and CD8 T-cell infiltration.
- **Key findings**: Paricalcitol safely administered; 42% on oral paricalcitol developed grade 2–4 hypercalcaemia; on-treatment biopsies showed reduced αSMA+ fibroblasts and increased CD8 T-cell co-localisation.
- **Strengths**: Randomised design with serial pharmacodynamic biopsies directly testing the stromal-reprogramming hypothesis in patients.
- **Limitations**: Small N (n=36), run-in phase not powered for efficacy; dose-limiting hypercalcaemia with oral formulation.
- **Conclusion**: Paricalcitol modulates the pancreatic tumour microenvironment as predicted by preclinical models, supporting larger efficacy trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s43018-026-01165-8 / PMID:42185478

## 4. Autologous T Cell Antigen Coupler Targeting HER2 (TAC01-HER2) in Advanced or Metastatic Solid Tumors — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology (Ann Oncol)
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Dumbrava EE et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety, feasibility, and early efficacy of TAC01-HER2, an autologous T-cell product with a novel receptor targeting HER2 in advanced solid tumours.
- **Method**: Phase 1 dose-escalation and dose-expansion trial; N=23 HER2+ solid tumour patients; RP2D 6–8 × 10⁶ cells/kg.
- **Key findings**: No treatment-related deaths; disease control rate 61.1% (18 evaluable patients); median PFS 2.6 months; 6-month OS 57.9% (95% CI 36.3–76.9%).
- **Strengths**: First human data on TAC construct — a CAR-T-distinct modality recruiting the native TCR — with manageable toxicity profile.
- **Limitations**: Small N (n=23), early phase 1 with limited follow-up; no randomised comparator.
- **Conclusion**: TAC01-HER2 shows acceptable safety and promising early signals in HER2+ gastric/GEJ cancers, warranting further investigation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.696 / PMID:42177028

## 5. Sequential chemo-immunotherapy followed by standard versus reduced thoracic radiotherapy for older and/or frail stage III non-small-cell lung cancer: A randomized open-label cohort trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: PLoS Medicine (PLoS Med)
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-27
- **Authors**: Qi WX et al.
- **Aims**: Assess feasibility and tolerability of reduced-dose RT with sequential chemo-immunotherapy for older/frail unresectable stage III NSCLC ineligible for concurrent CRT.
- **Method**: Phase II randomised open-label 2-cohort trial (N=56); primary endpoint 1-year PFS; 24-month median follow-up; registered NCT05557552.
- **Key findings**: 1-year PFS 84.3% (standard RT) vs 70.7% (reduced RT); grade 3/4 AEs 71.4% vs 53.6%; grade 5 AEs 10.7% vs 3.6%.
- **Strengths**: Prospective randomised design in an understudied frail/elderly population with pre-specified endpoints and adequate follow-up.
- **Limitations**: N=56, single-centre, non-comparative design, underpowered for non-inferiority or superiority conclusions.
- **Conclusion**: Reduced RT with sequential chemo-immunotherapy appears feasible; exploratory findings require confirmation in larger powered trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pmed.1005111 / PMID:42201929

## Appraisal notes

This window's selections reflect two converging themes: methodological innovation in causal inference and clinical trials addressing underserved cancer populations. The Stat Med paper advances semiparametric doubly-robust mediation estimation — a technique directly portable to cancer health-disparity research — while the Nat Cancer pancreatic RCT demonstrates how pharmacodynamic biopsy endpoints can de-risk stromal-targeting strategies before large efficacy trials. The PLoS Med NSCLC trial and BMJ NSC statement both address systemic gaps: the former targets older/frail patients excluded from landmark CRT trials, the latter tackles the long-standing tension between regulatory efficiency (surrogate endpoints) and clinical validity in cancer screening evaluation.
