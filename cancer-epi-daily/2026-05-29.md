## 1. Air pollution modifies clonal hematopoiesis-associated non-small cell lung cancer risk in non-smoking individuals — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Journal of the National Cancer Institute
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-27
- **Authors**: Buttigieg MM et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) synergizes with PM2.5 air pollution to promote NSCLC in never-smokers.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort study with meta-analysis across UK Biobank and All of Us (>650,000 participants); hazard ratios with interaction testing.
- **Key findings**: CHIP associated with NSCLC in never-smokers (HR=1.76 [1.07–2.89]); risk amplified with above-median PM2.5 (HR=2.51 [1.55–4.05]; p-interaction=0.02).
- **Strengths**: Two large independent cohorts; inflammatory biomarker (CRP, IL-6, IL-1β) data corroborate biological mechanism.
- **Limitations**: Observational design; CHIP status measured at single time point; residual confounding possible.
- **Conclusion**: CHIP × PM2.5 is a novel somatic gene × environment interaction promoting lung cancer tumorigenesis in non-smokers.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/jnci/djag159 / PMID:42202166

## 2. Predicting neoadjuvant breast cancer therapy response using BRIDGE from tumor transcriptomics and histopathology — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-26
- **Authors**: Cantore T et al.
- **Aims**: Develop BRIDGE, a computational framework deconvolving pre-treatment bulk transcriptomics to predict pathological complete response (pCR) to neoadjuvant therapy.
- **Method**: Transcriptomic deconvolution trained on 10 datasets, validated on 24 independent datasets; extended to histopathology slides via deep learning (BRIDGE-Slide).
- **Key findings**: ER+/HER2- AUC=0.84 (OR=8); HER2+ AUC=0.77 (OR=8.3); TNBC AUC=0.73 (OR=3.1). BRIDGE-Slide outperforms direct slide-to-response models.
- **Strengths**: Validated across 34 independent datasets spanning multiple subtypes; low-cost histopathology extension clinically actionable.
- **Limitations**: ICB-treated ER+/HER2- analyses remain exploratory; prospective validation of BRIDGE-Slide required.
- **Conclusion**: BRIDGE offers biologically grounded, multi-subtype pCR prediction with a deployable histopathology-based variant.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.700 / PMID:42203036

## 3. Neoadjuvant Durvalumab ± Tremelimumab in Combination With Dose-Dense MVAC in Muscle-Invasive Bladder Carcinoma: Results of the Phase I/II NEMIO Study — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-26
- **Authors**: Thibault C et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate efficacy and safety of neoadjuvant ddMVAC plus durvalumab ± tremelimumab in cisplatin-eligible muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC).
- **Method**: Multicenter randomized non-comparative phase I/II trial (NCT03549715); 119 patients; Bayesian analysis; co-primary endpoints pCR (≥45%) and grade ≥3 TRAEs (≤30%).
- **Key findings**: Bayesian pCR 48.7% doublet, 46.3% triplet; PD-L1-high pCR 68.3% vs 33.5% in PD-L1-low; 2-yr OS 85%/88%.
- **Strengths**: Randomized design with Bayesian framework; PD-L1 biomarker stratification; multicenter French cohort.
- **Limitations**: Non-comparative; triplet arm exceeded TRAE threshold (49.6% grade ≥3); small sample limits precision.
- **Conclusion**: ddMVAC + durvalumab shows encouraging pCR and survival; tremelimumab adds toxicity without efficacy benefit.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-03045 / PMID:42190158
