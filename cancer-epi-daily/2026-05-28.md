## 1. Toxicity-over-time analysis of anti-EGFR-based maintenance versus stop&go in left-sided, RAS/BRAF wild-type metastatic colorectal cancer — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Journal of the National Cancer Institute
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Raimondi A et al.
- **Aims**: Compare longitudinal toxicity profiles of three post-induction strategies in left-sided RAS/BRAF wt metastatic colorectal cancer using individual patient data.
- **Method**: IPD pooled analysis of 3 randomised phase II trials (PanaMa, Valentino, PRODIGE-28 TIME) using Toxicity-over-Time (ToxT) longitudinal adverse-event methodology.
- **Key findings**: Stop&go showed consistently lower anti-EGFR skin toxicity across all cycles vs. maintenance arms. Grade 3/4 AEs were more frequent in maintenance groups (n=327 total).
- **Strengths**: Novel ToxT methodology captures time dimension of AEs; IPD enables individual-level longitudinal comparison across three trials.
- **Limitations**: Indirect comparison; small stop&go arm (n=52) vs. maintenance groups (n=166/109).
- **Conclusion**: Stop&go yields a more favourable toxicity profile; shared decision-making should guide de-escalation strategy selection.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/jnci/djag160 / PMID:42178223

## 2. Gemcitabine and nab-paclitaxel with or without the VDR agonist paricalcitol for metastatic pancreatic cancer: a randomized, multiarm, run-in phase trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-25
- **Authors**: Perez KJ et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety and pharmacodynamic activity of adding the VDR agonist paricalcitol to first-line gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel in metastatic pancreatic cancer.
- **Method**: Randomised multiarm run-in trial (n=36); pre/on-treatment biopsies; primary endpoint safety; NCT03520790.
- **Key findings**: Paricalcitol safely combined with chemotherapy; on-treatment biopsies showed reduced αSMA+ fibroblasts and increased CD8+ T cell density. VDR expression predicted tumour response.
- **Strengths**: Paired serial biopsies with spatial and proteomic analyses provide direct pharmacodynamic evidence of TME remodelling.
- **Limitations**: Small n=36 run-in design; 42% grade 2–4 hypercalcaemia with oral paricalcitol requiring dose reduction.
- **Conclusion**: Paricalcitol is safe with GA chemotherapy and modulates the TME consistent with preclinical predictions; larger efficacy trials warranted.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s43018-026-01165-8 / PMID:42185478

## 3. Tumor irradiation promotes antigen dressing of dendritic cells to enhance CAR T cell persistence and efficacy in lung metastases — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Navarre S et al.
- **Aims**: Define the mechanistic basis by which tumour irradiation selectively enhances CAR T cell activity at metastatic lung tumour sites.
- **Method**: Syngeneic mouse models of metastatic lung adenocarcinoma and melanoma; 8 Gy irradiation + CAR T cell therapy; spatial and flow cytometry analyses.
- **Key findings**: 8 Gy irradiation enhanced CAR T persistence via DC-mediated trogocytic antigen dressing. Tumour control improved without increased toxicity in normal tissue expressing the same antigen.
- **Strengths**: DC depletion experiments confirm mechanism; selectivity for tumour vs. on-target/off-tumour toxicity is a key clinical safety advance.
- **Limitations**: Preclinical syngeneic models only; clinical translation requires human validation.
- **Conclusion**: DC-mediated antigen dressing provides a mechanistic rationale and potential dosing strategy for combining radiotherapy with CAR T cell therapy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s43018-026-01167-6 / PMID:42174275

## 4. Concurrent genetic and non-genetic resistance mechanisms to KRAS inhibition in colorectal cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Cell
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Alonso S et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise concurrent genetic and transcriptional mechanisms of resistance to KRAS+EGFR inhibitor treatment in colorectal cancer patients.
- **Method**: Targeted exome sequencing and spatial transcriptomics on patient-matched pre/post-treatment CRC biopsies; human and murine organoid models.
- **Key findings**: Acquired genetic events (subclonal) co-exist with mesenchymal/YAP/fetal transcriptional adaptive states. TBK1 identified as target to block inflammatory adaptive phase.
- **Strengths**: Patient-matched longitudinal biopsies with spatial single-cell resolution; functional TBK1 validation in organoid models.
- **Limitations**: Subclonal genetic heterogeneity complicates resistance attribution; limited patient cohort size.
- **Conclusion**: Concurrent dual-mechanism resistance underscores need for combination strategies; TBK1 inhibition may enhance KRAS inhibitor durability.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.ccell.2026.04.009 / PMID:42167227

## 5. Autologous T Cell Antigen Coupler Targeting HER2 (TAC01-HER2) in Advanced or Metastatic Solid Tumors — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Dumbrava EE et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety, feasibility, and early efficacy of TAC01-HER2 autologous T cell therapy in HER2-positive advanced solid tumours.
- **Method**: Phase 1 dose-escalation and expansion trial; n=23 HER2+ solid tumour patients; RP2D 6–8×10⁶/kg.
- **Key findings**: No treatment-related deaths; 2/9 gastric/GEJ patients achieved partial response; disease control rate 61.1%; 6-month OS 57.9% (95% CI 36.3–76.9%).
- **Strengths**: First-in-human TAC receptor data demonstrating favourable CRS profile vs. prior CAR-T approaches in HER2+ disease.
- **Limitations**: Small n=23 single-arm phase 1; early-stage efficacy data only.
- **Conclusion**: TAC01-HER2 shows manageable toxicity and early signals of efficacy in HER2+ gastric/GEJ cancers, supporting phase 2 investigation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.696 / PMID:42177028
