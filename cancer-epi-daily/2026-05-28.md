## 1. Confidence Set for the Cluster of a Spatial Scan Statistic — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Statistics in Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: Lee J et al.
- **Aims**: Propose a novel framework for constructing confidence sets that quantify and visualise uncertainty around detected spatial cancer clusters.
- **Method**: Likelihood-based confidence sets derived from likelihood ratio test p-values; binary search algorithm for efficiency; applied to Poisson and Normal models on cancer mortality data.
- **Key findings**: Simulation studies confirm empirical coverage near nominal levels across non-circular and multiple-cluster scenarios; confidence heatmaps and envelopes demonstrate practical utility on real cancer mortality data.
- **Strengths**: Fills a critical gap in spatial epidemiology — first principled framework for cluster uncertainty quantification with two novel visualisation tools.
- **Limitations**: Current implementation focuses on single-cluster detection; extensions to multiple clusters and spatio-temporal analysis remain future work.
- **Conclusion**: Framework advances spatial cancer cluster analysis by quantifying uncertainty rather than relying solely on p-value significance.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1002/sim.70590 / PMID:42179328

## 2. Temporal Dynamics of Prognostic Factors in Breast Cancer Survival — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-27
- **Authors**: Tvete IF et al.
- **Aims**: Examine time-varying prognostic impacts of molecular subtype and radiotherapy on breast-cancer-specific mortality in a national registry cohort.
- **Method**: Time-varying coefficient cause-specific Cox models; Cancer Registry of Norway; 36,412 women diagnosed 2006–2020 with competing risks framework.
- **Key findings**: HR−/HER2+ patients had HR 3.38 vs HR+/HER2− within 3 years post-diagnosis, falling to 0.93 thereafter; radiation therapy benefit was highest in early years and diminished substantially later.
- **Strengths**: Large national registry (36,412 women, 14-year follow-up); competing risks framework separating BC-specific and other-cause mortality reveals effects hidden by standard models.
- **Limitations**: Observational registry; residual confounding by treatment intensity and comorbidities cannot be excluded.
- **Conclusion**: Proportional hazards models miss clinically important time-varying effects of molecular subtype and radiotherapy on long-term breast cancer survival.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0349115 / PMID:42201883

## 3. Fibroblast Growth Factor Receptor Inhibition for Succinate Dehydrogenase-Deficient Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors: A Phase 2 Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-26
- **Authors**: Merriam P et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate efficacy of pan-FGFR inhibitor rogaratinib in advanced SDH-deficient GIST, where epigenetic hypermethylation activates an FGF3/FGF4–FGFR1 autocrine oncogenic loop.
- **Method**: Phase 2 single-arm trial (NCT04595747); 24 patients with SDH-deficient GIST; primary endpoint objective response rate; whole-exome sequencing on serial biopsies.
- **Key findings**: ORR 41.7% (10/24 partial responses); median PFS 31.0 months (95% CI 20.2–NR); 1-year PFS 77.4% (95% CI 61.7–97.1%).
- **Strengths**: First targeted therapy trial for this rare epigenetically-driven subtype; biologic mechanism validated by serial biopsies, pharmacodynamics, and WES.
- **Limitations**: Single-arm, 24 patients, rare disease context; no OS data yet; no control arm.
- **Conclusion**: Rogaratinib achieves durable responses in SDH-deficient GIST, establishing FGFR inhibition as a viable epigenetically-targeted oncology strategy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04376-9 / PMID:42191879

## 4. Mediation Analysis With Bayesian Nonlinear Joint Models: Evaluation of Treatment Causal Pathways Between Tumor Growth Kinetics and Overall Survival — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Statistics in Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: Kazantzidis G et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a Bayesian joint modelling framework to quantify the extent to which tumor size dynamics mediate treatment effects on overall survival in oncology trials.
- **Method**: Bayesian hierarchical joint model combining nonlinear tumor growth inhibition sub-model with parametric survival model; PTE, NDE, NIE estimated over time; applied to IMBrave150 Phase III.
- **Key findings**: In IMBrave150, mediated proportion of treatment effect varied over time; tumor dynamics partially but not fully explain the survival benefit, challenging tumour size as a sole surrogate.
- **Strengths**: Novel integration of causal mediation theory with tumor kinetics; simulation validation across varied mediation levels and sample sizes confirms low bias and near-nominal coverage.
- **Limitations**: Computationally intensive Bayesian estimation; surrogate endpoint validity requires cross-tumour-type external replication.
- **Conclusion**: Framework provides a principled causal mediation tool for oncology surrogacy evaluation, showing tumor dynamics are a partial mediator of treatment benefit in HCC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1002/sim.70574 / PMID:42179334

## 5. Local Genetic Correlations Between Systemic Sclerosis and Common Cancer Types — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-27
- **Authors**: Patasova K et al.
- **Aims**: Identify fine-scaled pleiotropic loci between systemic sclerosis (SSc) and SSc-associated cancers (breast, lung, hematologic) using local genetic correlation analysis.
- **Method**: GWAS summary statistics; LDSC for global correlations; LAVA for local correlations; colocalization and fine-mapping; SSc vs breast cancer subtypes, lung cancer, lymphoma/CLL.
- **Key findings**: No significant global genetic correlation detected; 23 significant local bivariate correlations (21 with breast cancer subtypes); one confirmed pleiotropic locus between SSc and lung cancer implicating ECM remodelling genes.
- **Strengths**: Demonstrates that locus-specific approaches detect pleiotropy missed by genome-wide summaries; multi-method triangulation (colocalization, fine-mapping, enrichment analysis).
- **Limitations**: Findings are statistical associations; functional validation and causal inference require additional studies; limited to available GWAS summary statistics.
- **Conclusion**: A pleiotropic SSc–lung cancer locus involving cell communication and ECM remodelling pathways provides gene candidates for mechanistic follow-up.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0350006 / PMID:42201917

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest is dominated by methodological innovation in cancer epidemiology: two Stat Med papers introduce novel statistical frameworks (spatial cluster confidence sets; Bayesian causal mediation for tumour kinetics), while the registry and GWAS papers deploy time-varying survival models and local genetic correlation analysis to address long-standing epi questions. A recurring theme is the inadequacy of standard assumptions — proportional hazards, global genetic correlation, and tumour-size surrogacy — each challenged or refined by the methods presented this week. The Nat Med phase 2 trial stands out for translating an epigenetic discovery into durable clinical responses in a rare GIST subtype, illustrating how mechanistic cancer biology can align with epi-methodological principles of causal pathway analysis.
