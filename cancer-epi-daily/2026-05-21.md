## 1. Mitochondrial DNA Breaks and Copy Number and the Risk of Lung Cancer in the Shanghai Women's Health Study — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Blechter B et al.
- **Aims**: Examine associations between mtDNA fragmentation, copy number, and lung cancer risk in never-smoking women.
- **Method**: Nested case-control (n=789 cases, 789 controls) within prospective Shanghai Women's Health Study; quantitative PCR for mtDNA fraction with breaks and copy number.
- **Key findings**: Higher mtDNA fragmentation inversely associated with lung cancer (OR=0.66, 95% CI: 0.51–0.85); significant interaction with mtDNA copy number (P=8.3×10⁻⁶).
- **Strengths**: Large prospective cohort with prediagnostic samples; novel simultaneous measurement of two mtDNA metrics.
- **Limitations**: Single ethnic population (Chinese women); biological mechanism of inverse association unclear.
- **Conclusion**: Lower mtDNA fragmentation signals increased lung cancer risk, suggesting mitochondrial stress response as a novel carcinogenesis biomarker.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0047 / PMID:42165669

## 2. Prostate Cancer Mortality After Relabeling Low-Grade Prostate Cancer as Precancerous — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Vickers AJ et al.
- **Aims**: Model net effect of relabeling grade group 1 prostate cancer as a precancerous condition on US prostate cancer mortality.
- **Method**: Decision analytical model using US population-based and clinical data (2020–2025); extensive sensitivity analyses across competing scenarios.
- **Key findings**: Base case: ~6× more deaths avoided than caused annually (2835 vs 452); net mortality reduction robust across most model scenarios.
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive sensitivity analysis; directly addresses overdiagnosis/overtreatment vs screening trade-off policy debate.
- **Limitations**: Assumes behavioural responses to relabeling; actual screening uptake changes are inherently uncertain.
- **Conclusion**: Dropping the cancer label from GG1 prostate disease is projected to reduce net prostate cancer mortality in the US.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1391 / PMID:42166158

## 3. Intraperitoneal and Intravenous Paclitaxel Plus S-1 for Gastric Cancer With Peritoneal Metastasis: A Phase 3 Randomized Clinical Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Yan C et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether adding intraperitoneal (IP) paclitaxel to IV paclitaxel plus S-1 improves overall survival in gastric cancer with peritoneal metastasis.
- **Method**: Multicenter open-label phase 3 RCT (DRAGON-01); 9 hospitals in China; n=222; 2:1 randomisation; median follow-up ~72 months.
- **Key findings**: Median OS 19.4 vs 13.9 months (HR=0.67, 95% CI 0.50–0.90; P=.01); grade 3/4 adverse events comparable between arms.
- **Strengths**: First positive phase 3 RCT of IP paclitaxel for gastric cancer peritoneal metastasis; very long follow-up (~6 years).
- **Limitations**: Single-country (China) study; no immunotherapy comparator; open-label design.
- **Conclusion**: Adding IP paclitaxel to IV paclitaxel plus S-1 significantly improves OS without increased severe toxicity in gastric cancer with peritoneal metastasis.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1347 / PMID:42166134

## 4. Bladder Adjuvant Radiotherapy: Phase III Multicenter Randomized Controlled Trial of Adjuvant Radiotherapy or Observation for Postcystectomy Muscle-Invasive Bladder Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Murthy V et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate adjuvant pelvic image-guided IMRT after radical cystectomy in high-risk muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC).
- **Method**: Multicenter phase 3 RCT; n=153; 1:1 randomisation to IG-IMRT 50.4 Gy vs observation; stratified by nodal status and chemotherapy; primary endpoint: 2-year locoregional recurrence-free survival.
- **Key findings**: 2-year LRFS 87.1% vs 76.0% (HR=0.43, 95% CI 0.20–0.96; P=.04); DFS trend favouring RT (71.6% vs 58.7%).
- **Strengths**: First phase 3 RCT addressing adjuvant RT in MIBC; image-guided IMRT technique minimises toxicity.
- **Limitations**: Modest sample size (n=153); no immunotherapy era comparator; OS difference not yet significant.
- **Conclusion**: Adjuvant pelvic IMRT improves locoregional control post-cystectomy without additional severe toxicity in high-risk MIBC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-02093 / PMID:42166701
