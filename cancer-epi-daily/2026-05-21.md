## 1. Prostate Cancer Mortality After Relabeling Low-Grade Prostate Cancer as Precancerous — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Vickers AJ et al.
- **Aims**: Model the net mortality effect of reclassifying grade group 1 (GG1) prostate cancer as a precancerous lesion in the US.
- **Method**: Decision analytical model using US population-based and clinical data (2020–2025); compared annual deaths averted via increased PSA screening vs. deaths caused by reduced active-surveillance adherence.
- **Key findings**: Base case: relabeling avoids ~2835 deaths/year vs. causes ~452 (6-fold net benefit). Net mortality reduction held when absolute screening increase was only 3%.
- **Strengths**: Extensive scenario analyses across wide assumption ranges demonstrated robust conclusions.
- **Limitations**: Model-based; actual post-relabeling behavioral changes in screening and surveillance are uncertain.
- **Conclusion**: Dropping the cancer label from GG1 prostate disease is predicted to yield a net reduction in US prostate cancer mortality.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1391 / PMID:42166158

## 2. Mitochondrial DNA Breaks and Copy Number and the Risk of Lung Cancer in the Shanghai Women's Health Study — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Blechter B et al.
- **Aims**: Investigate prediagnostic mtDNA fragmentation (mtDNAfb) and copy number (mtDNAcn) as lung cancer risk biomarkers in never-smoking women.
- **Method**: Nested case-control study (n=789 matched pairs) within the prospective Shanghai Women's Health Study; prediagnostic whole-blood qPCR; conditional logistic regression.
- **Key findings**: High mtDNAfb was inversely associated with lung cancer risk (OR=0.66, 95% CI 0.51–0.85); interaction with mtDNAcn was highly significant (P=8.3×10⁻⁶).
- **Strengths**: Prediagnostic blood samples in a prospective cohort minimise reverse causation bias.
- **Limitations**: Single cohort of never-smoking Asian women; generalisability to other populations uncertain.
- **Conclusion**: Lower mtDNA fragmentation is associated with increased lung cancer risk, particularly among women with low mtDNA copy number, implicating impaired mitochondrial stress response in lung carcinogenesis.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0047 / PMID:42165669

## 3. Riesz Representers for the Rest of Us — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Epidemiology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Williams NT et al.
- **Aims**: Introduce the Riesz representation theorem and Riesz regression to applied epidemiologists for use with semiparametric efficient estimators.
- **Method**: Pedagogical paper with step-by-step worked examples illustrating Riesz representers in causal inference and machine-learning-augmented estimation settings.
- **Key findings**: Riesz regression provides a unified, model-agnostic approach to nuisance function estimation; worked examples demonstrate practical epidemiologic application.
- **Strengths**: Bridges advanced semiparametric theory and applied practice with reproducible, concrete worked examples.
- **Limitations**: Primarily pedagogical; empirical performance of Riesz regression in diverse real-world epi settings not evaluated.
- **Conclusion**: Riesz representers unify a growing class of semiparametric efficient estimators and offer epidemiologists tractable tools for modern causal inference.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1097/EDE.0000000000001996 / PMID:42165509

## 4. Asynchronous Evolution of Epithelium and Stroma Differentiates Precursor Lesions from Pancreatic Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Discov
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-21
- **Authors**: Elhossiny AM et al.
- **Aims**: Map how the tumor microenvironment evolves from pancreatic intraepithelial neoplasia (PanIN) to invasive pancreatic cancer.
- **Method**: Spatial transcriptomics and genomics on rare donor-organ PanIN samples compared to malignant pancreas; myeloid/fibroblast/lymphocyte deconvolution across progression stages.
- **Key findings**: PanIN epithelium lies on a continuum with cancer, but PanIN stroma is drastically distinct; a cancer-specific SMA+/LRRC15+/LEF1+ fibroblast population arises only with malignancy.
- **Strengths**: Unconfounded access to genuine precancer tissue via donor organs, avoiding co-contamination by adjacent malignancy.
- **Limitations**: Sample size constrained by rarity of donor-organ PanIN tissue; progression inferred cross-sectionally.
- **Conclusion**: Stromal reprogramming, not epithelial progression alone, may gatekeep PanIN-to-cancer transition, explaining why most PanINs remain indolent.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/2159-8290.CD-25-2001 / PMID:42165710

## 5. Longitudinal Transcriptomic Profiling Identifies Predictors of Response to Neoadjuvant Chemoimmunotherapy in Triple-Negative Breast Cancer: Results from the NeoTRIPaPDL1 Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Ann Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-19
- **Authors**: Dugo M et al.
- **Aims**: Identify dynamic molecular predictors of pathologic complete response (pCR) to neoadjuvant chemoimmunotherapy (atezolizumab + carboplatin/nab-paclitaxel) in high-risk TNBC.
- **Method**: Phase III NeoTRIPaPDL1 RCT (n=280); longitudinal RNA-seq at baseline and day 1 cycle 2; univariable logistic regression and multivariable XGBoost.
- **Key findings**: Absence of tumor cells in early on-treatment biopsy strongly predicted surgical pCR in both arms; on-treatment immune activation surpassed baseline metabolic signatures as dominant pCR correlate.
- **Strengths**: Longitudinal sampling embedded in a well-powered Phase III RCT captures dynamic tumour-microenvironment remodelling.
- **Limitations**: On-treatment biopsy requirement limits scalability; multivariable findings are exploratory and require prospective validation.
- **Conclusion**: Early tumour clearance on biopsy is a strong pCR surrogate supporting response-adapted neoadjuvant strategies in TNBC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.694 / PMID:42162664
