## 1. Treatment-related adverse events of CD3-based T cell-engaging bispecific antibodies in patients with cancer: a meta-analysis of clinical trials — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Zhu Y et al.
- **Aims**: Systematically characterise incidence and patterns of treatment-related adverse events for CD3-based bispecific antibodies across cancer types.
- **Method**: Systematic review and random-effects meta-analysis of 104 clinical trials (PROSPERO-registered), logit-transformed pooled estimates with 95% CIs.
- **Key findings**: All-grade adverse events occurred in 97·5% (haematological) and 97·9% (solid tumours); grade ≥3 events in 70·3% vs 45·3%; treatment-related death rate 1·0% across 9,206 patients.
- **Strengths**: Large sample (10,353 patients), pre-registered, comprehensive cross-cancer-type comparison with heterogeneity quantification.
- **Limitations**: High heterogeneity (I² up to 94%) across trials limits precise pooled estimates; publication bias not reported.
- **Conclusion**: Toxicity profile varies substantially by cancer type and antibody class, informing patient management as these agents enter routine practice.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00086-0 / PMID:42285121

## 2. Spatial biomarker discovery via interpretable semantic learning in histopathology — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Cell
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Liang J et al.
- **Aims**: Develop an interpretable AI framework to systematically discover spatial tissue biomarkers from whole-slide images for colorectal cancer prognosis and treatment stratification.
- **Method**: PathPrism encodes histological tissue architecture into interpretable spatial features; validated across 11 cohorts (7,000 patients with CRC), with VirtualWSI for semantic perturbation experiments.
- **Key findings**: Hundreds of spatial biomarkers predicted survival, MSI, BRAF/TP53 mutations, and chemotherapy benefit in stage II/III CRC; framework outperformed black-box models in interpretability benchmarks.
- **Strengths**: Multi-cohort validation (n=7,000), transparent feature encoding, enables virtual experimentation within spatial biomarker atlas.
- **Limitations**: External validation limited to CRC; LLM-generated hypotheses require prospective experimental verification.
- **Conclusion**: PathPrism offers a scalable interpretable approach for spatial biomarker discovery that could transform precision oncology trial stratification.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.ccell.2026.05.014 / PMID:42276049

## 3. Defining Cardiovascular Endpoints in Oncology Trials: Challenges and Opportunities — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-15
- **Authors**: Barac A et al.
- **Aims**: Provide a harmonised framework for selecting and adjudicating cardiovascular endpoints in contemporary oncology clinical trials.
- **Method**: American Heart Association scientific statement; expert consensus linking drug-specific mechanisms to endpoint selection, with standardised adverse cardiovascular event definitions.
- **Key findings**: Proposes unified definitions for heart failure, arrhythmias, myocarditis, and thrombotic events; aligns with CTCAE and patient-reported outcomes; addresses competing risks and late toxicities.
- **Strengths**: Directly addresses regulatory gap; harmonises cardio-oncology trial endpoints across FDA, AHA, and industry frameworks.
- **Limitations**: Expert consensus without empirical validation; uptake depends on voluntary adoption by trial sponsors and regulators.
- **Conclusion**: Standardising cardiovascular endpoint assessment across oncology trials will improve risk stratification and support regulatory acceptance of new cancer therapies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-01647 / PMID:42296457
