## 1. Updated Results of the POSITIVE Trial: Pregnancy Outcome and Safety of Interrupting Endocrine Therapy in HR+ Early Breast Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Pagani O et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether temporary interruption of adjuvant endocrine therapy to attempt pregnancy is safe in young HR+ early breast cancer patients at extended follow-up.
- **Method**: Pre-planned analysis of POSITIVE single-arm prospective multicenter trial (n=518); bootstrap-matching to SOFT/TEXT external controls; median follow-up 71 months.
- **Key findings**: 5-year BCFI event rate 12.3% vs 13.2% in controls (−0.9%, 95% CI −4.2% to 2.6%); 5-year DRFI 6.2% vs 8.3%; 76% of women achieved documented pregnancy.
- **Strengths**: Large international cohort, pre-planned extended follow-up, validated bootstrap-matched external comparator from SOFT/TEXT.
- **Limitations**: Single-arm design; residual confounding from bootstrap-matching cannot be excluded; late recurrence risk requires continued follow-up.
- **Conclusion**: Temporary ET interruption for pregnancy does not increase breast cancer recurrence risk at median 71-month follow-up.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.699 / PMID:42214557

## 2. Neoadjuvant Durvalumab ± Tremelimumab With ddMVAC in Muscle-Invasive Bladder Carcinoma: NEMIO Phase I/II Trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Journal of Clinical Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-26
- **Authors**: Thibault C et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate efficacy and safety of neoadjuvant ddMVAC combined with durvalumab ± tremelimumab in cisplatin-eligible MIBC.
- **Method**: Multicenter randomized noncomparative phase I/II trial (n=119); Bayesian posterior analysis; co-primary endpoints pCR (ypT0N0) and grade ≥3 TRAEs.
- **Key findings**: Bayesian posterior mean pCR 48.7% (doublet) and 46.3% (triplet); PD-L1-high tumors achieved 68.3% pCR vs 33.5% PD-L1-low; 2-year OS 85% and 88%.
- **Strengths**: Randomized design with Bayesian framework; exploratory PD-L1 biomarker analysis in 103 patients.
- **Limitations**: Non-comparative design precludes definitive superiority claims; adding tremelimumab increased toxicity without pCR benefit.
- **Conclusion**: ddMVAC plus durvalumab demonstrates encouraging pCR rates and manageable toxicity as neoadjuvant chemoimmunotherapy for MIBC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-03045 / PMID:42190158

## 3. Real-World Palbociclib as 1st- or 2nd-Line Therapy in HR+/HER2− Metastatic Breast Cancer: Multicenter Prospective Cohort — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Breast Cancer Research
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-29
- **Authors**: Ishikawa T et al.
- **Aims**: Compare PFS and treatment-sequence outcomes of 1st- versus 2nd-line palbociclib in HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer in clinical practice.
- **Method**: Prospective observational multicenter cohort (n=593), April 2019–January 2023; primary endpoint PFS; secondary PFS2 from start of 1st-line endocrine therapy.
- **Key findings**: Median PFS 25.8, 18.0, and 12.0 months for 1st, 2nd, 3rd-line; PFS2 was 36.9 months (1st-line) vs 57.9 months (2nd-line), favouring 2nd-line sequencing.
- **Strengths**: Prospective design captures real-world practice; large multicentre sample; includes full treatment-sequence PFS2 endpoint.
- **Limitations**: Observational design with potential selection bias; RCT benchmark comparison is indirect.
- **Conclusion**: Real-world evidence supports that deferring CDK4/6 inhibitor to 2nd-line may yield longer cumulative time to second progression for some patients.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s13058-026-02313-9 / PMID:42216212

## 4. Atezolizumab vs Pembrolizumab in Advanced Triple-Negative Breast Cancer: Bayesian Network Meta-Analysis — Score 3/6

- **Journal**: BMC Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-29
- **Authors**: Wiguna IGWW et al.
- **Aims**: Indirectly compare atezolizumab + chemotherapy vs pembrolizumab + chemotherapy in advanced TNBC using network meta-analysis.
- **Method**: Bayesian NMA of 4 phase III RCTs (n=2,776); outcomes include ORR, DCR, landmark mortality and progression probability ratios, RMST; PRISMA 2020.
- **Key findings**: Atezolizumab + CT showed higher ORR in PD-L1-positive patients; pembrolizumab + CT demonstrated more durable long-term OS in ITT (SUCRA ranking); GRADE certainty low-to-moderate.
- **Strengths**: Rigorous Bayesian framework; GRADE evidence appraisal; analysis stratified by ITT and PD-L1-positive populations.
- **Limitations**: Only 4 eligible RCTs; no direct head-to-head data; low-to-moderate GRADE certainty limits conclusions.
- **Conclusion**: Bayesian NMA suggests biomarker-stratified ICI selection, but head-to-head trials remain needed.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12885-026-16207-8 / PMID:42215937

## 5. Effects of Metformin on Head and Neck Cancer: An Umbrella Review — Score 3/6

- **Journal**: BMC Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-29
- **Authors**: Mehta V et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate the effectiveness of metformin among head and neck cancer patients by synthesising available systematic reviews.
- **Method**: Umbrella review; 3 databases + grey literature; random-effects meta-analysis; 5 studies included; PRISMA 2020 guidelines.
- **Key findings**: Metformin associated with 0.74-fold (95% CI 0.67–0.81) OS probability vs non-users; I²=0%; Egger's test indicated potential publication bias.
- **Strengths**: Umbrella review design; negligible heterogeneity (I²=0%); transparent PRISMA-compliant reporting.
- **Limitations**: Only 5 included studies; low confidence rating; potential publication bias; predominantly observational evidence base precludes causal inference.
- **Conclusion**: Metformin shows promising survival benefit in HNC, but evidence base is too limited for clinical recommendations.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12885-026-16057-4 / PMID:42215897
