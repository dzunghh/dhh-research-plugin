## 1. Development and validation of a multiancestry and multitrait polygenic risk score for lung cancer — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-27
- **Authors**: Zhang Y et al.
- **Aims**: Develop an accurate multiancestry + multitrait PRS for lung cancer to overcome ancestry imbalance in existing PRSs.
- **Method**: GWAS meta-analysis (76,953 cases, 1,886,372 controls); PRS-CSx for multiancestry PRS; CatBoost for multitrait integration; validated in OncoArray, TRICL, All of Us, and UK Biobank.
- **Key findings**: 87 conditionally independent loci (2 novel cytobands); individuals with high PRS + high PLCO score had 12.64-fold higher cumulative risk; ~10% additional UK Biobank cases identified.
- **Strengths**: Largest multiancestry lung cancer GWAS meta-analysis; independent validation in four diverse cohorts including All of Us.
- **Limitations**: European ancestry still dominates training data; African and Asian representation remains limited.
- **Conclusion**: Multiancestry + multitrait PRS substantially outperforms existing lung cancer PRSs across ancestries, supporting precision prevention programs.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74964-3 / PMID:42364979

## 2. Risk stratification and relapse pattern in TNBC with pathological complete response after neoadjuvant treatment: the European GAMBIT real-world study — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Massa D et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether baseline stromal TILs stratify residual relapse risk among TNBC patients achieving pCR after neoadjuvant therapy.
- **Method**: Multicentric retrospective real-world study (n=2457 TNBC/ER-low HER2-negative); pCR subgroup n=1192; evaluable TIL n=690; survival analysis with TIL and nodal status.
- **Key findings**: Node-positive/low-TIL pCR patients had 5-yr DRFS 83.4% and OS 85.8%; 5-yr CNS cumulative incidence 7.5% (6.9% isolated CNS relapse) in high-risk subgroup.
- **Strengths**: Large European multicenter cohort; TIL + nodal status interaction provides actionable risk stratification beyond pCR.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective design; evaluable TILs available in only 690/1192 pCR patients.
- **Conclusion**: TILs plus nodal status identify a high-risk pCR subgroup warranting CNS-directed risk-adapted trial strategies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74056-2 / PMID:42343076

## 3. Variable selection-combined causal mediation analysis for continuous treatments with application to large-dimensional biomedical data — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: PLoS Computational Biology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Zhou Y et al.
- **Aims**: Extend causal mediation analysis to continuous treatment/mediator settings with high-dimensional covariate selection using UK Biobank data.
- **Method**: Weighted semiparametric framework integrating outcome-adaptive LASSO into generalized propensity score modeling; simulation study; applied to UK Biobank (diabetes risk → ApoB → cancer incidence).
- **Key findings**: Outperforms other regularization methods in selection accuracy and estimation efficiency; quantifies significant ApoB-mediated causal pathway from diabetes risk to cancer incidence.
- **Strengths**: Novel methodological framework directly applicable to large-scale biobank epi studies; open-source implementation.
- **Limitations**: Relies on untestable no-unmeasured-confounding assumption; continuous mediation interpretation is complex.
- **Conclusion**: Weighted semiparametric mediation with outcome-adaptive LASSO advances high-dimensional causal epi for biobank-scale studies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pcbi.1014436 / PMID:42341064

## 4. The metabolic vulnerability index predicts outcomes in patients with metabolic dysfunction associated steatotic liver disease — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-23
- **Authors**: Siddiqui MS et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate the prognostic performance of the metabolic vulnerability index (MVX) for liver-related outcomes including hepatocellular cancer in MASLD.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort (n=1613), full histological spectrum of MASLD, median 4-year follow-up; Cox regression and AUROC for all-cause mortality, liver-related mortality, HCC, and hepatic decompensation.
- **Key findings**: MVX predicts all-cause mortality (HR 2.7 per 10-point increment); MVX + fibrosis improves HCC AUROC from 0.78 to 0.84 vs fibrosis alone (p=0.001).
- **Strengths**: Large prospective cohort with full histological characterisation; multiple clinically relevant outcomes including HCC.
- **Limitations**: Single-country cohort; no external validation dataset for HCC-specific MVX performance.
- **Conclusion**: MVX combined with fibrosis stage improves risk stratification for HCC and liver-related mortality in MASLD, supporting its development as a clinical prognostic tool.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73742-5 / PMID:42337268

## 5. Multimodal blood-based proteomic profiling reveals insights into mechanisms of immunotherapy resistance — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Wright SJ et al.
- **Aims**: Identify circulating biomarkers of primary resistance or early progression on immune checkpoint blockade in metastatic melanoma.
- **Method**: Plasma proteomics (>2900 proteins) + high-dimensional mass cytometry across serial time points in 250 metastatic melanoma patients on ICB; 92 patient-matched tumors with scRNA-seq/bulk RNA-seq.
- **Key findings**: ICB-upregulated proteins associate with effector immune activation; non-responder proteins highest in suppressive myeloid subsets and malignant cells; responder epithelial proteins link to immune-related toxicity.
- **Strengths**: Multi-modal design with serial sampling and paired tumor-blood data; largest such proteomic cohort in melanoma ICB.
- **Limitations**: Single-centre retrospective study; no independent validation cohort for identified biomarkers.
- **Conclusion**: Peripheral multimodal proteomic profiling captures systemic immune-tumor dynamics of ICB response and identifies candidate blood-based resistance biomarkers.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74790-7 / PMID:42342704

## Appraisal notes

This week's picks highlight two convergent methodological trends: genetic risk stratification at scale (multiancestry PRS for lung cancer; causal mediation in UK Biobank) and real-world cohort epi of treatment response (GAMBIT TNBC, n=2457). The MASLD→HCC prognostic cohort and the melanoma ICB proteomic study extend cancer epi into the metabolic-liver-cancer and immuno-oncology spaces respectively, reflecting growing interest in non-traditional cancer risk exposures and blood-based biomarker discovery for personalised prevention.
