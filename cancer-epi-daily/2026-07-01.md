## 1. Targeting Homologous Recombination Deficiency with Intensified Chemotherapy Versus Standard Chemotherapy Plus Olaparib in Stage III Breast Cancer (SUBITO) — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-07-01
- **Authors**: Seefat RL et al.
- **Aims**: Prospectively compare intensified alkylating chemotherapy with stem cell rescue (IACT) versus carboplatin-paclitaxel plus olaparib in HRD HER2-negative stage III breast cancer.
- **Method**: Phase 3 open-label RCT (n=174; 10 centres, Netherlands and France); primary endpoint 4-year overall survival, ITT analysis.
- **Key findings**: 4-year OS was 77.0% (IACT) vs 76.4% (olaparib); HR 1.11 (95% CI 0.57–2.17; p=0.37). Grade 3–4 toxicity substantially higher with IACT.
- **Strengths**: First prospective RCT overturning a post-hoc survival advantage for IACT; molecularly selected HRD population enables precision trial design.
- **Limitations**: Small sample (n=174); limited power for formal equivalence; median follow-up 41 months may miss late divergence.
- **Conclusion**: IACT confers no survival advantage over olaparib-based regimen in HRD stage III breast cancer, with substantially greater toxicity.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00131-2 / PMID:42372741

## 2. Tailoring Radiotherapy in cT1-2N1 Breast Cancer to Nodal Response After Primary Chemotherapy (RAPCHEM: BOOG 2010-03) — 10-Year Follow-Up — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-07-01
- **Authors**: Mauritz AJW et al.
- **Aims**: Present pre-specified 10-year secondary endpoints of response-adapted locoregional radiotherapy in cT1-2N1 (≤3 nodes) breast cancer after primary systemic therapy.
- **Method**: Prospective multi-centre registry study; 17 Dutch radiation oncology centres; 838 patients stratified into three nodal-response risk groups.
- **Key findings**: 10-year locoregional recurrence rate 2.9% overall (2.4% low-risk, 3.2% intermediate, 2.8% high-risk). 10-year OS 83.0%; no between-group differences.
- **Strengths**: Large prospective registry with 10-year follow-up confirming durable safety of de-escalated radiotherapy guided by nodal pathological response.
- **Limitations**: Non-randomised registry; potential for residual confounding in patient allocation across risk groups.
- **Conclusion**: Response-tailored locoregional radiotherapy achieves low 10-year locoregional recurrence and excellent survival, supporting de-escalation in appropriately selected patients.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00216-0 / PMID:42372742

## 3. Internal Mammary and Medial Supraclavicular Irradiation in Stage I-III Breast Cancer (EORTC 22922/10925): 20-Year Outcomes in Node-Negative Patients — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-07-01
- **Authors**: Kaidar-Person O et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate 20-year impact of internal mammary and medial supraclavicular (IM-MS) nodal irradiation in the pN0 subset of the EORTC 22922/10925 RCT.
- **Method**: Unplanned subset analysis of phase 3 RCT (pN0 subset n=1778; 46 centres, 13 countries); median follow-up 22.2 years.
- **Key findings**: Breast cancer mortality HR 0.70 (p=0.010) in favour of IM-MS irradiation; non-breast cancer mortality HR 1.24 (p=0.048) offsetting the OS benefit.
- **Strengths**: Exceptionally long follow-up (22 years) from a large international RCT; uniquely captures late non-cancer mortality from irradiation.
- **Limitations**: Unplanned subset analysis susceptible to type I error; older irradiation techniques differ from modern dose-sparing approaches.
- **Conclusion**: IM-MS irradiation lowers breast cancer mortality at 20 years in pN0 patients but excess non-cancer deaths abolish overall survival benefit.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00233-0 / PMID:42372743
