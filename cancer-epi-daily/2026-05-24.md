## 1. Biomarker-Based Eligibility for Lung Cancer Screening: Validation of the Protein-Based INTEGRAL-Risk Model — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-18
- **Authors**: Zahed H et al.
- **Aims**: Develop and validate a protein-based risk model to improve lung cancer screening eligibility beyond USPSTF 2021 criteria.
- **Method**: Case-cohort study (14 international cohorts, n=3,695); 13-protein panel trained in 7 cohorts, validated in 7 independent cohorts.
- **Key findings**: INTEGRAL-Risk AUC 0.88 vs PLCOm2012 0.79 at 1 year (p<0.001); captures 85% of lung cancers vs 63% with USPSTF 2021 criteria.
- **Strengths**: Multi-cohort international design with independent validation; well-calibrated (E/O 0.87 over 3 years).
- **Limitations**: Blood samples collected 1985–2009; contemporary prospective validation of the protein panel still needed.
- **Conclusion**: Protein biomarkers substantially improve lung cancer risk prediction, potentially expanding screening to missed high-risk individuals.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jama.2026.8044 / PMID:42149699

## 2. Genetic Analysis of Circulating Metabolic Traits in 619,372 Individuals — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-20
- **Authors**: Tambets R et al.
- **Aims**: Characterize the genetic architecture of 249 circulating metabolic traits and explore causal disease links via Mendelian randomization.
- **Method**: GWAS meta-analysis (Estonian Biobank + UK Biobank, n=619,372); cis-Mendelian randomization; statistical fine-mapping; phenome-wide colocalization.
- **Key findings**: 88,127 locus-trait associations from 8,398 loci; BCAA lowering unlikely to reduce T2D risk; 19.4% of fine-mapped variants had MAF 0.1–1%.
- **Strengths**: Largest metabolomics GWAS to date; dual-biobank replication enables systematic causal inference across metabolic-disease pairs.
- **Limitations**: Predominantly European ancestry; metabolomics platform heterogeneity between the two biobanks.
- **Conclusion**: Integrating low-frequency variants with metabolic traits and MR challenges observational associations such as BCAA and type 2 diabetes risk.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41586-026-10532-5 / PMID:42162431

## 3. Pathogenic Germline Variations and Cancer Risks in Pediatric Patients Referred for Genetic Testing — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-20
- **Authors**: Wang H et al.
- **Aims**: Determine cancer predisposition and incidence in pediatric patients with germline pathogenic or likely pathogenic (P/LP) variants on exome sequencing.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort of 75,602 pediatric patients with exome sequencing; 64,187 initially tumor-free followed prospectively for new malignancies.
- **Key findings**: 32.6% of pediatric tumor patients had causative germline P/LP variants; malignant tumor incidence 3.23 vs 0.24–0.27/1,000 person-years in non-carriers.
- **Strengths**: Very large prospective pediatric cohort with quantitative cancer incidence rates enabling clinical risk communication.
- **Limitations**: Conducted in Chinese pediatric hospitals; genetic testing referral bias limits population generalizability.
- **Conclusion**: Pediatric P/LP variant carriers face >10-fold higher cancer incidence, supporting proactive genetic counselling and surveillance.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04423-5 / PMID:42162371

## 4. Real-World Heart and Lung Doses from 30,000 NHS Radiotherapy Treatment Plans in England: A National Audit of Breast Radiotherapy Practice — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-18
- **Authors**: Kirby AM et al.
- **Aims**: Characterize real-world heart and lung doses in NHS England breast radiotherapy and identify inter-center variation.
- **Method**: National audit of 30,582 radiotherapy plans from 48/49 NHS England centers via cloud-based dosimetry repository; cross-sectional analysis vs trial-derived constraints.
- **Key findings**: 99.8% of left-sided breast-only plans met mean heart dose <2 Gy; VMAT gave better target coverage but higher heart doses than field-based techniques.
- **Strengths**: Near-complete national sample (48/49 centers, >30,000 plans); high real-world generalizability.
- **Limitations**: No patient-level clinical outcome data; cross-sectional snapshot cannot assess temporal trends.
- **Conclusion**: NHS breast radiotherapy meets cardiac dose constraints near-universally, but VMAT planning variation identifies quality-improvement opportunities.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00185-3 / PMID:42150588

## 5. Long-Term Outcomes of Stereotactic Ablative Body Radiotherapy for Primary Kidney Cancer (TROG 15.03 FASTRACK II): A Multicentre, Non-Randomised, Phase 2 Study — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-17
- **Authors**: Siva S et al.
- **Aims**: Report definitive 5-year outcomes of SABR for medically inoperable primary renal cell carcinoma.
- **Method**: Multi-center non-randomized phase 2 trial (8 hospitals, Australia/Netherlands); 70 patients; median follow-up 62 months; SABR 26 Gy single or 42 Gy × 3 fractions.
- **Key findings**: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months; 10% grade 3 adverse events; no grade 4 events or treatment-related deaths.
- **Strengths**: Longest prospective follow-up (62 months median) for renal SABR; multi-center, multi-national design.
- **Limitations**: Non-randomized, small sample (70 patients), and no surgical comparator arm.
- **Conclusion**: SABR provides durable 5-year local control for inoperable renal cell carcinoma, supporting evaluation in a randomized trial.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4 / PMID:42144018
