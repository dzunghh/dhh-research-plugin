## 1. Antibody-Drug Conjugates for Locally Advanced and Metastatic Urothelial Carcinoma: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Network Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Zhang W et al.
- **Aims**: Synthesise multidimensional evidence on enfortumab vedotin, disitamab vedotin, and sacituzumab govitecan outcomes in la/mUC across interventional and observational settings.
- **Method**: Systematic review + Bayesian network meta-analysis of 40 studies (n=6,085); IPD reconstruction; meta-regression for heterogeneity drivers; PRISMA with deduplication algorithm.
- **Key findings**: EV+pembrolizumab pooled ORR 67.5% (95% CI 63.5–71.3%); EV monotherapy OS advantage vs chemotherapy (HR 0.50, 95% CI 0.29–0.86) in first-line cisplatin-ineligible patients.
- **Strengths**: Bayesian NMA with reconstructed individual patient data; meta-regression identifies prior immunotherapy exposure and hepatic burden as response modifiers.
- **Limitations**: Substantial heterogeneity across observational and interventional settings; limited disitamab vedotin data from non-Asian populations.
- **Conclusion**: EV-based regimens show robust outcomes across settings; prior PD-1/L1 exposure and hepatic burden attenuate treatment response.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.14764 / PMID:42223943

## 2. Digital Self-Management of Symptoms and Quality of Life for Patients With Advanced Cancer: A Randomized Clinical Trial — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Network Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Chan WL et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether app-facilitated symptom monitoring plus nurse-led follow-up improves HRQOL and reduces acute care use in advanced cancer patients off systemic therapy.
- **Method**: Multicenter RCT, 6 palliative care clinics (Hong Kong), n=1,214, 18-week follow-up; primary outcome EQ-5D-5L utility; pre-registered (NCT07475312).
- **Key findings**: Digital monitoring preserved HRQOL vs usual care (EQ-5D utility difference 0.15, 95% CI 0.10–0.21, P<0.001); unplanned hospitalizations lower (17.2% vs 28.5%, OR 1.59).
- **Strengths**: Large multicenter pre-registered RCT; patient-reported and healthcare utilisation outcomes; caregiver engagement captured.
- **Limitations**: Single health system (Hong Kong); 67.6% caregiver-as-app-user limits individual patient generalisability; no treatment blinding.
- **Conclusion**: App-facilitated palliative monitoring with nurse-led alerts maintains HRQOL and meaningfully reduces unplanned hospitalizations in advanced cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.15509 / PMID:42224222

## 3. Aglatimagene Besadenovec (CAN-2409) With Radiotherapy for Patients With Localised Prostate Cancer: A Phase 3, Multicentre, Randomised, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: DeWeese TL et al.
- **Aims**: Assess whether adding intraprostatic aglatimagene gene therapy plus valacyclovir to EBRT improves disease-free survival in intermediate/high-risk localised prostate cancer.
- **Method**: Double-blind placebo-controlled phase 3 RCT, 51 US centres, n=745; median follow-up 50.3 months; primary endpoint DFS by intention-to-treat.
- **Key findings**: DFS significantly improved with aglatimagene (HR 0.70, 95% CI 0.52–0.94, p=0.016); median DFS not reached vs 86.1 months for placebo; grade ≥3 AEs similar (8% vs 7%).
- **Strengths**: Large double-blind phase 3 design; stratified randomisation; community and institutional centres included.
- **Limitations**: 79% White cohort; no overall survival data yet at median 50-month follow-up; industry-sponsored.
- **Conclusion**: Aglatimagene plus valacyclovir added to radiotherapy significantly extends DFS without meaningful added toxicity in localised prostate cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00071-9 / PMID:42225101

## 4. [Lu]Lu-DOTA-TATE Versus Sunitinib in Patients With Metastatic Progressive Neuroendocrine Tumours of the Pancreas (OCLURANDOM): A Randomised, Controlled, Phase 2 Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: Baudin E et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate antitumour activity and safety of [177Lu]Lu-DOTA-TATE versus sunitinib in pretreated, somatostatin receptor-positive metastatic pancreatic neuroendocrine tumours.
- **Method**: Open-label randomised non-comparative phase 2 trial, 10 French centres, n=84, crossover allowed; primary endpoint 12-month PFS by central RECIST review; median follow-up 72.5 months.
- **Key findings**: 12-month PFS rate 80.5% (90% CI 67.5–89.9%) for Lu-DOTATATE vs 41.9% for sunitinib; grade 3–4 AEs lower (44% vs 72%); QoL favoured Lu-DOTATATE (+10.3-point GHS score).
- **Strengths**: First randomised comparison of PRRT vs standard-of-care in pNETs; long follow-up; central imaging review.
- **Limitations**: Small sample (n=84); non-comparative design limits formal statistical inference; late AEs (grade ≥2) in 60% of PRRT arm; crossover complicates OS interpretation.
- **Conclusion**: Lu-DOTATATE demonstrates superior 12-month PFS over sunitinib with better tolerability and QoL, supporting PRRT as a key second-line option in pNETs.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00108-7 / PMID:42225102

## 5. Lymph Node Dissection and Postoperative Complications After Lung Cancer Resection — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Network Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Madeka I et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether compliance with the guideline-concordant 3+1 lymph node sampling rule is associated with increased postoperative complications in NSCLC.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort using STS General Thoracic Database (n=28,439 clinical T1-T3N0M0 NSCLC); propensity-score matching to compare 3+1-compliant vs non-compliant sampling.
- **Key findings**: 3+1 compliant patients had longer OR time (224 vs 210 min, P<0.001) and higher pathologic upstaging (13.3% vs 9.7%); no difference in composite complications after propensity matching.
- **Strengths**: Very large national registry; propensity matching; broad 14-component complication composite including 30-day mortality and readmission.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective design; potential coding errors in registry; no long-term oncologic follow-up reported.
- **Conclusion**: The 3+1 lymph node sampling rule is not associated with excess postoperative complications, supporting continued guideline-concordant adoption.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.15894 / PMID:42223938
