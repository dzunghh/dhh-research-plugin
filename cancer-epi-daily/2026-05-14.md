## Appraisal notes

Today's digest is anchored by the NordICC 13-year colonoscopy RCT update (Lancet), which delivers the field's clearest signal yet that population screening reduces CRC incidence but has not—after more than a decade—translated into a mortality benefit, reinforcing debates about adherence-adjusted estimands and the role of interval cancers. Complementing this, the Lancet Oncology microsimulation and Nature Medicine PAF analyses converge on the same urgent theme: the burden of preventable and undiagnosed cancer falls disproportionately on low- and middle-income countries, with tobacco, infectious agents, and obesity collectively responsible for more than one-third of global cases. The Statistics in Medicine MR framework paper provides timely methodological scaffolding for the field's growing reliance on Mendelian randomisation to test causal cancer-risk hypotheses.
