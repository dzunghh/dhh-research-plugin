## 1. Effect of [Ga]Ga-PSMA-11 PET-CT in the diagnosis of prostate cancer in men with equivocal or clinically high-risk non-suspicious findings on multiparametric MRI (PRIMARY2) — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-10
- **Authors**: Buteau JP et al.
- **Aims**: To test whether PSMA-PET-CT can safely replace systematic biopsy for men with high clinical risk but equivocal/non-suspicious prostate MRI.
- **Method**: Multicentre phase 3 non-inferiority RCT (7 Australian hospitals, n=660); PSMA-PET-CT vs. systematic transperineal biopsy, ITT analysis.
- **Key findings**: Clinically significant prostate cancer detection was non-inferior (12% PET-CT vs. 16% biopsy; difference −3.7%, 95% CI −8.9 to 1.5%). PSMA-PET avoided biopsy in 49% of the experimental arm (p<0.0001).
- **Strengths**: Well-powered RCT with pre-specified non-inferiority margin; multicentre; prospective registration (NCT05154162).
- **Limitations**: Ethnicity data not collected; generalisability to non-PSMA radiopharmaceuticals and non-Australian settings unclear.
- **Conclusion**: PSMA-PET could safely triage nearly half of high-risk men with equivocal MRI away from biopsy without compromising cancer detection.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00120-8 / PMID:42269655

## 2. Temporal Trends of Subsequent Malignant Neoplasms in Childhood Cancer Survivors and the Impact of Treatment Changes: A DCCSS-LATER Study — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: J Natl Cancer Inst
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Westerveld ASR et al.
- **Aims**: To evaluate whether changes in childhood cancer treatment have reduced subsequent malignant neoplasm (SMN) risk over six decades.
- **Method**: Dutch national cohort (DCCSS-LATER); 10,612 five-year survivors (1963–2014); multivariable Cox regression; median follow-up 20.4 years.
- **Key findings**: SMN risk declined over time (p-trend=0.04), driven by reduced radiotherapy use (HR 2.3, 95% CI 1.9–2.8). Increased anthracycline and epipodophyllotoxin use partially offset these gains (p-trend<0.001).
- **Strengths**: Largest Dutch childhood survivor cohort; national registry linkage; long follow-up spanning multiple treatment eras.
- **Limitations**: Residual confounding by evolving diagnostic sensitivity; single-country sample limits international generalisability.
- **Conclusion**: Radiotherapy reduction has meaningfully lowered SMN risk in childhood cancer survivors, but chemotherapy intensification partially counteracts this benefit.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/jnci/djag169 / PMID:42286208

## 3. Fecal immunochemical tests from population-based colorectal cancer screening programs support prospective microbiome cohorts — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Br J Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Byrd DA et al.
- **Aims**: To leverage residual FIT stool from a CRC screening programme to characterise the gut microbiome across the adenoma–carcinoma sequence.
- **Method**: Population-based case-control study in Turin, Italy; n=925 (44 CRC, 269 advanced adenoma, 134 early adenoma, 478 FIT-negative controls); whole-genome shotgun sequencing; multivariable logistic regression.
- **Key findings**: Alpha diversity was inversely associated with colorectal neoplasms (OR 0.45 for early adenoma, 95% CI 0.25–0.80). Parvimonas micra, E. coli, and B. fragilis were associated with higher odds across neoplasm stages.
- **Strengths**: Large population-based sample; spans full adenoma-carcinoma continuum; novel use of FIT residuals as scalable biobank resource.
- **Limitations**: Single-centre Italian cohort; cross-sectional FIT sample limits causal inference; n=44 CRC cases may limit statistical power.
- **Conclusion**: CRC screening infrastructure can serve as a cost-efficient platform for large-scale microbiome cancer-risk epidemiology.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03495-x / PMID:42286232

## 4. How Wide is the Screening Window for Aggressive Non-Small Cell Lung Cancers? The Cure Threshold Metric and its Implications for Lung Cancer Screening Guidelines — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Goldwasser DL et al.
- **Aims**: To quantify the surgical cure threshold for aggressive NSCLC and assess its implications for LDCT screening mortality benefit.
- **Method**: Analytical-modelling synthesis of NLST, NELSON trial data, and historical registry estimates; re-estimation of median tumour size at metastatic transition.
- **Key findings**: Contemporary evidence supports a median surgical cure threshold <20 mm, approximately half of prior registry-based estimates. LDCT benefit is highly sensitive to small-nodule work-up.
- **Strengths**: Addresses a critical methodological assumption underlying screening guidelines; integrates multiple data sources.
- **Limitations**: Modelling study with assumptions inherited from existing trials; no original cohort data; single author.
- **Conclusion**: The screening window for aggressive NSCLC may be far narrower than assumed, suggesting current guidelines undervalue early small-nodule work-up.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0440 / PMID:42283747

## 5. Extracellular matrix biomarkers of T-cell infiltration and tumor fibrosis predict response to nivolumab ± ipilimumab with SBRT in biliary tract cancer: insights from the CheckPAC trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: BMC Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Rasmussen MB et al.
- **Aims**: To evaluate serological ECM biomarkers as predictors of immunotherapy response and overall survival in metastatic biliary tract cancer.
- **Method**: Prospective biomarker sub-study of the CheckPAC RCT (NCT02866383); n=61 patients receiving SBRT + nivolumab ± ipilimumab; multivariable Cox regression; landmark analysis.
- **Key findings**: Baseline PRO-C3 (tumour fibrosis marker) independently predicted shorter OS (multivariable HR significant). Longitudinal C4G increase (cytotoxic T-cell activity marker) was associated with clinical benefit (p=0.007) and longer OS (p=0.0045).
- **Strengths**: Prospective design; multivariable adjustment; novel liquid-biopsy ECM panel covering fibrosis and immune infiltration simultaneously.
- **Limitations**: Small n (61); single-arm pharmacodynamic sub-study; independent validation cohort absent.
- **Conclusion**: Serum ECM biomarkers reflecting fibrosis and T-cell activity show promise for early pharmacodynamic monitoring in biliary tract cancer immunotherapy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12916-026-04989-4 / PMID:42277831

## Appraisal notes

This week's crop reflects three converging methodological themes: the maturation of theranostic imaging as a randomised trial endpoint (PRIMARY2's PSMA-PET RCT in Lancet Oncol is a landmark for precision diagnosis replacing invasive biopsy), the use of large national registries to disentangle treatment-era effects on long-term cancer risk (DCCSS-LATER demonstrating chemotherapy now partially offsets radiotherapy gains), and the exploitation of screening-programme infrastructure for cancer-microbiome epidemiology (Turin FIT cohort setting a template for scalable biobank studies). Papers 4 and 5 are methodologically interesting but limited by modelling assumptions and small cohort size respectively; the shortlist would have benefited from more Mendelian randomisation or large GWAS-epi studies, which were absent in this 7-day window.
