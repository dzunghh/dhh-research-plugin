## 1. Healthier pre-diagnosis lifestyle and long-term survival in colorectal cancer: Evidence from the Singapore Chinese Health Study — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Ho PJ et al.
- **Aims**: Quantify survival differences in incident CRC patients by composite pre-diagnosis lifestyle score in an Asian cohort.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort (Singapore Chinese Health Study); n=2124 CRC cases; registry-linked deaths through December 2022; Cox models and restricted mean survival time (RMST).
- **Key findings**: High vs. low lifestyle score: HRadj 0.74 (95%CI 0.65–0.92); 10-year RMST difference 0.85 years (95%CI 0.34–1.35); stage dominated with RMST difference −3.33 years (late vs. early).
- **Strengths**: Large prospective Asian cohort with composite lifestyle score and RMST for absolute survival differences.
- **Limitations**: Single enrolment-period exposure assessment; residual confounding from unmeasured factors possible.
- **Conclusion**: Healthier pre-diagnosis lifestyle modestly improves long-term CRC survival, but stage at diagnosis remains the primary determinant.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0308 / PMID:42274337

## 2. Robust discovery of mutational signatures using power posteriors — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: PLoS Computational Biology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Xue C et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a Bayesian NMF method robust to model misspecification for discovering cancer mutational signatures.
- **Method**: BayesPowerNMF — Bayesian non-negative matrix factorization with power posteriors; extensive simulations and applied to ICGC/TCGA Pan-Cancer data across 6 cancer types.
- **Key findings**: BayesPowerNMF recovered more true signatures with higher accuracy than all leading methods in simulations; outperformed current state-of-the-art on real whole-genome sequencing data.
- **Strengths**: Principled Bayesian framework with automated latent-factor selection and full uncertainty quantification.
- **Limitations**: Computational demands not benchmarked; application demonstrated on only six cancer types.
- **Conclusion**: Power-posterior NMF provides a more robust and accurate approach to mutational signature discovery, with direct implications for cancer etiology and targeted therapy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pcbi.1014372 / PMID:42275462

## 3. Association of common genetic alterations with tumor recurrence in papillary thyroid cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Journal of the National Cancer Institute
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Yu ST et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether common genetic alterations predict recurrence in papillary thyroid cancer (PTC) and improve ATA risk stratification.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort; 2,056 PTC patients from 3 Chinese centres (2020–2024); median follow-up 25 months; Cox regression adjusted for clinicopathological factors.
- **Key findings**: RET fusions: HRadj 2.64 (95%CI 1.31–5.32); genetic alteration combination: HR 3.37 (95%CI 1.21–9.35); integrating genetics into ATA 2025 RSS markedly improved discrimination (e.g., HR 14.02 in low-risk category).
- **Strengths**: Multi-centre, comprehensive evaluation of all major PTC mutations tested against the updated ATA risk system.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective, single-country; short median follow-up of 25 months; no external validation cohort.
- **Conclusion**: RET fusions and combined genetic alterations are independent prognostic markers in PTC; integration into ATA 2025 RSS substantially improves recurrence risk stratification.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/jnci/djag190 / PMID:42286408

## 4. Cost-effectiveness analysis of mammography screening for early detection of breast cancer in Nigeria — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-12
- **Authors**: Nduka IJ et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate cost-effectiveness of annual vs. biennial mammography screening for Nigerian women aged ≥40, to inform national policy.
- **Method**: State-transition Markov model; lifetime horizon; DALY averted as primary outcome; ICER from healthcare provider perspective; 5% discount rate.
- **Key findings**: Annual mammography ICER = US$207.24/DALY averted (95%CI US$213.31–US$216.88), below WTP threshold of US$1,074; annual was most cost-effective strategy.
- **Strengths**: Policy-relevant analysis for a Sub-Saharan African setting with no national screening programme.
- **Limitations**: Model-dependent assumptions; healthcare provider perspective may underestimate full societal costs.
- **Conclusion**: Annual mammography is cost-effective from the healthcare provider perspective in Nigeria, supporting consideration of a national breast cancer screening programme.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0351492 / PMID:42284308

## 5. Economic evaluation of atezolizumab in combination with bevacizumab and chemotherapy for metastatic, persistent, or recurrent cervical cancer in China — Score 3/6

- **Journal**: PLoS One
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-11
- **Authors**: Yang S et al.
- **Aims**: Assess cost-effectiveness of atezolizumab + bevacizumab + chemotherapy vs. bevacizumab + chemotherapy for cervical cancer from the Chinese healthcare perspective.
- **Method**: Markov model with 3-week cycles, 10-year horizon; survival data from BEATcc RCT; outcomes: QALYs, ICER; WTP threshold US$36,859 (China).
- **Key findings**: ICER = US$432,597/QALY gained, exceeding WTP threshold by ~12-fold; treatment not cost-effective from Chinese payer perspective.
- **Strengths**: Based on phase III RCT data; deterministic and probabilistic sensitivity analyses confirm robustness.
- **Limitations**: Cost and utility inputs sourced from published literature rather than primary data; single-country perspective limits generalisability.
- **Conclusion**: Atezolizumab combination is not cost-effective for metastatic/recurrent cervical cancer in China at current pricing, highlighting affordability barriers.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1371/journal.pone.0351029 / PMID:42275400
