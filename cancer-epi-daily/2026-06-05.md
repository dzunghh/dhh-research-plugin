## 1. Cost-Effectiveness of Fecal Immunochemical Testing Alone vs Co-Testing With Helicobacter pylori Stool Antigen — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: JAMA
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Lee YC et al.
- **Aims**: Project lifetime health benefits and costs of adding H. pylori stool antigen testing to biennial FIT screening for gastric and colorectal cancer prevention.
- **Method**: Lifetime Markov decision-analytic cost-effectiveness model, societal perspective, calibrated to a pragmatic RCT (n=~35,000) in Taiwan; 30-year horizon with probabilistic sensitivity analysis.
- **Key findings**: Co-testing dominated FIT alone: base-case cost-saving ICER of $2,094/QALY gained (95% CI, $12,359 saved to $7,291 additional cost); 5-fold benefit-cost ratio in Taiwan. In the USA, co-testing remained cost-effective at willingness-to-pay ≤$100,000/QALY when H pylori prevalence ≥21.9%.
- **Strengths**: Grounded in real-world pragmatic RCT with incomplete adherence; dual-cancer outcome framework; comprehensive one-way and probabilistic sensitivity analyses.
- **Limitations**: Taiwan-specific prevalence and cost parameters limit direct global transferability; long-term gastric cancer mortality reductions modelled, not observed.
- **Conclusion**: Population-based H pylori stool antigen co-testing with FIT is cost-saving in high-prevalence settings and cost-effective in moderate-prevalence settings, supporting expanded screening programmes.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jama.2026.6908 / PMID:42223961

## 2. Chemotherapy for Patients With Circulating Tumour DNA Positive, Stage II Colon Cancer (CIRCULATE) — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Ann Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-12-31 (EDAT: 2026-06-05)
- **Authors**: Folprecht G et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether postoperative ctDNA status can guide adjuvant chemotherapy decisions in stage II pMMR/MSS colon cancer.
- **Method**: Randomised trial (Germany/Austria); ctDNA-positive patients 2:1 to capecitabine ± oxaliplatin vs observation; ctDNA-negative 1:4 to observation vs off-study; tumour-informed NGS ctDNA assay; primary endpoint disease-free survival.
- **Key findings**: ctDNA positivity strongly prognostic (3-year DFS 52% vs 87%, HR 4.28 [2.32–7.93], p<0.001). Per-protocol analysis: chemotherapy reduced 3-year recurrence (19% vs 62%, HR 0.23 [0.06–0.87], p=0.009), though primary ITT endpoint not met.
- **Strengths**: Largest European ctDNA-guided stage II colon cancer study (2,126 screened); academic NGS assay; long follow-up; blinded ctDNA allocation for observation arm.
- **Limitations**: Trial ended early due to funding (only 26 patients on chemo arm), severely underpowering ITT analysis; 2.9% ctDNA positivity rate lower than prior estimates.
- **Conclusion**: ctDNA positivity identifies a high-risk subgroup with strong per-protocol evidence of chemotherapy benefit, supporting ctDNA-guided adjuvant decision-making pending confirmatory trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.001 / PMID:42225236

## 3. Fovinaciclib for First-Line Therapy of Advanced Breast Cancer: A Randomized Clinical Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Yuan P et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate efficacy and safety of fovinaciclib (novel CDK inhibitor) plus aromatase inhibitor as first-line treatment for HR+/HER2− advanced breast cancer.
- **Method**: Phase 3 double-blind RCT; 417 patients; 63 centres in China; fovinaciclib 200 mg vs placebo on days 1–21 of 28-day cycles plus letrozole or anastrozole; primary endpoint PFS by blinded independent central review.
- **Key findings**: Median PFS not reached vs 20.2 months (HR 0.55 [95% CI 0.38–0.77], 1-sided p<0.001); consistent PFS benefit across subgroups; grade ≥3 haematologic AEs not leading to discontinuation; only 1.4% discontinuation rate in each arm.
- **Strengths**: Phase 3, double-blind, pre-specified interim analysis, blinded independent review; quality-of-life data included; no increase in discontinuations vs placebo.
- **Limitations**: OS data immature (9.6% events); conducted entirely in China; generalisability to other populations unconfirmed.
- **Conclusion**: Fovinaciclib plus aromatase inhibitor confers clinically meaningful first-line PFS benefit with manageable toxicity, adding a new CDK-inhibitor option for HR+/HER2− advanced breast cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1938 / PMID:42224659

## 4. SEZ6-Targeting Antibody-Drug Conjugate ABBV-706 in Advanced Small Cell Lung Cancer and Solid Tumors: A Phase 1 Trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nat Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Byers LA et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety, tolerability, PK, and antitumour activity of ABBV-706 (SEZ6-directed ADC with Top1 inhibitor payload) in advanced solid tumours including R/R SCLC.
- **Method**: Open-label phase 1 trial (dose escalation, optimisation, expansion); 288 patients total; 124 R/R SCLC; primary endpoints: safety, RP2D determination.
- **Key findings**: ORR 52% (65/124) in R/R SCLC monotherapy; median OS 12.4 months at 1.8 mg/kg RP2D; grade ≥3 TRAEs in 61%; anemia (61%) and fatigue (38%) most common any-grade TRAEs.
- **Strengths**: Large phase 1 cohort for an early-stage trial; multiple expansion cohorts; long-term efficacy measures included in RP2D determination; ORR markedly exceeds historical 2nd-line SCLC benchmarks.
- **Limitations**: No randomised control; phase 1 design; SEZ6 expression testing not fully standardised; late-line heavily pre-treated population.
- **Conclusion**: ABBV-706 demonstrates compelling anti-tumour activity in R/R SCLC with 1.8 mg/kg Q3W confirmed as RP2D, supporting advancement to phase 2/3 trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04452-0 / PMID:42225988

## 5. Savolitinib in MET-Amplified Gastric or Gastroesophageal Junction Adenocarcinoma: A Phase 2 Trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nat Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01
- **Authors**: Peng Z et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate antitumour activity of savolitinib (oral MET inhibitor) in MET-amplified, locally advanced or metastatic gastric/GEJ cancer.
- **Method**: Open-label, multicenter phase 2 trial (China); exploratory phase (n=45) plus pivotal phase (n=65, gene copy number ≥10); primary endpoint ORR by independent review in pivotal cohort.
- **Key findings**: Pivotal-phase ORR 32.3% (95% CI 21.2–45.1%), exceeding the predefined ≥15% lower bound; grade ≥3 TRAEs in 34.5%; one treatment-related death (0.9%).
- **Strengths**: Pre-defined statistical success threshold met; centralised biomarker selection (METamp GCN ≥10); multinational oncology centres; ORR meets meaningful benchmark in a difficult-to-treat biomarker-selected subgroup.
- **Limitations**: Single arm, no comparator; enriched for later-line, heavily pre-treated patients; generalisability to first-line settings uncertain.
- **Conclusion**: Savolitinib shows meaningful anti-tumour activity in METamp G/GEJ cancer with acceptable safety, supporting further investigation in randomised trials with MET-biomarker selection.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04459-7 / PMID:42225990
