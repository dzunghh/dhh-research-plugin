## 1. Cancer workforce — a global crisis: a Lancet Oncology Commission — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: Hricak H et al.
- **Aims**: Provide actionable guidance for strengthening the global cancer workforce, emphasising LMIC disparities.
- **Method**: Burden modelling of 17 cancers and 18 workforce types globally; survival and workforce projections to 2050.
- **Key findings**: One in three cancers go undiagnosed worldwide; >60% in parts of Africa. Global workforce shortage projected at ~100 million by 2050 (nurses 65M, diagnostics 16M). Comprehensive scale-up could avert 170 million cancer deaths and yield US$120 trillion net economic benefit (2030–2050).
- **Strengths**: Largest multi-cancer, multi-workforce global projection to date with actionable policy recommendations.
- **Limitations**: Relies on model projections with uncertain input data quality, particularly in LMICs.
- **Conclusion**: Urgent cross-sector workforce investment in LMICs is essential to close the global cancer care gap.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00065-3 / PMID:42218903

## 2. Impact of Comorbidities on Survival among American Indian and Alaska Native People with Cancer: A SEER-Medicare Study — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-02
- **Authors**: Nash SH et al.
- **Aims**: Explore predictors of cancer survival among AIAN people with focus on the contribution of comorbidities to observed disparities.
- **Method**: SEER-Medicare linked data (2000–2019); multivariable Cox PH models; comorbidity classes derived by latent class analysis; breast, colorectal, and lung cancers.
- **Key findings**: Comorbidity class predicted 5-year all-cause mortality for breast and colorectal but not lung cancer. Comorbidity prevalence did not explain cancer-specific survival disparities in AIAN people vs. other groups.
- **Strengths**: Population-based SEER-Medicare linkage with latent class-based comorbidity profiling enables nuanced characterisation beyond simple Charlson scores.
- **Limitations**: Restricted to Medicare-eligible participants (≥66 years), limiting generalisability to younger AIAN populations.
- **Conclusion**: Structural and access factors beyond comorbidity burden must be prioritised to address AIAN cancer survival disparities.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0182 / PMID:42227960

## 3. Randomized Interventional Effects in Semicompeting Risks, With Application to a Hematopoietic Cell Transplantation Study — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Stat Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06
- **Authors**: Deng Y et al.
- **Aims**: Extend the randomised interventional framework to time-to-event outcomes under semicompeting risks for causal mediation in cancer studies.
- **Method**: New identification framework via random draw from reference intermediate-event distribution; nonparametric maximum likelihood estimation; latent frailty sensitivity analysis; applied to matched unrelated vs. haploidentical donor HCT in lymphoma with GVHD as time-varying confounder.
- **Key findings**: Matched unrelated donor transplantation was preferable for survival after accounting for relapse-mediated effects with GVHD as time-varying confounder. Framework valid under both marginal and conditional reference distributions.
- **Strengths**: Rigorous causal mediation framework for semicompeting risks; includes sensitivity analysis for unmeasured confounding.
- **Limitations**: Requires strong positivity assumptions for nonparametric estimation; implementation complexity may limit uptake.
- **Conclusion**: This new framework enables principled causal mediation analysis in survival settings with competing and intermediate events.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1002/sim.70628 / PMID:42227732

## 4. A randomised study of encorafenib, cetuximab, and FOLFIRI versus FOLFIRI with or without bevacizumab in BRAF V600E-mutant colorectal cancer: BREAKWATER Cohort 3 — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Ann Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: Kopetz S et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate encorafenib + cetuximab + FOLFIRI (EC+FOLFIRI) versus FOLFIRI±bevacizumab as first-line therapy in BRAF V600E-mutant metastatic CRC.
- **Method**: Randomised phase 3 trial (1:1); BICR-assessed ORR as primary endpoint; PFS as key secondary; n=147.
- **Key findings**: EC+FOLFIRI ORR 64.4% vs 39.2% (OR 2.76; p=0.0011). Median PFS 15.2 vs 8.3 months (HR 0.44; p=0.0002). OS trend HR 0.56 (NE vs 20.3 months).
- **Strengths**: Randomised design with BICR; confirms a new first-line standard of care with stratified molecular selection.
- **Limitations**: Optional bevacizumab in control arm introduces heterogeneity; modest sample size (n=147).
- **Conclusion**: EC+FOLFIRI significantly improves ORR and PFS over FOLFIRI±bevacizumab, establishing a new first-line standard for BRAF V600E mCRC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.04.017 / PMID:42219860

## 5. Amivantamab in recurrent/metastatic HNSCC after checkpoint inhibitor and chemotherapy: pivotal results from the phase 1b/2 OrigAMI-4 study — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: Burtness B et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate subcutaneous amivantamab (EGFR-MET bispecific antibody) in R/M HNSCC after PD-(L)1 inhibitor and platinum-based chemotherapy.
- **Method**: Phase 1b/2 single-arm study (Cohort 1), n=102; primary endpoint RECIST v1.1 ORR by BICR; median follow-up 11.8 months.
- **Key findings**: BICR ORR 42% (95% CI 32–52%), complete response rate 15%; median DoR not reached; 56% of responses ≥6 months. Median OS 12.5 months.
- **Strengths**: ORR nearly double historical benchmarks for paclitaxel/cetuximab (21–24%); low treatment-related discontinuation rate (8%).
- **Limitations**: Single-arm design without randomised comparator; prior anti-EGFR was exclusionary, limiting generalisability.
- **Conclusion**: Amivantamab demonstrated substantially greater antitumor activity than historical comparators in heavily pre-treated R/M HNSCC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-26-01042 / PMID:42218660

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest is anchored by a major global epidemiological modelling exercise (Lancet Oncol Commission) projecting a 100-million workforce shortfall and identifying underdiagnosis as the dominant bottleneck in LMIC cancer care. The SEER-Medicare study on AIAN cancer survival adds precision to a persistent disparity question: comorbidities are not the driver, redirecting research toward structural access barriers. On the methods front, the *Stat Med* semicompeting risks paper provides a timely causal mediation tool directly applicable to cancer trial endpoints where intermediate events (relapse, GVHD) precede death—a methodologically underserved area. The two clinical trials (BREAKWATER Cohort 3 and OrigAMI-4) represent high-impact phase 3 and pivotal phase 1b/2 confirmatory data for molecularly selected populations in CRC and HNSCC respectively.
