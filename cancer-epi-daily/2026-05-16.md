## Appraisal notes

The most consequential paper this cycle is the NordICC 13-year update (Lancet), which confirms that a single colonoscopy reduces CRC incidence by ~19–30% yet fails to show a significant mortality benefit — a tension that challenges current CRC screening guidelines and will likely catalyse policy debate. The JAMA Network Open SEER analysis and Lancet Oncology simulation together highlight a shared signal: cancer burden is rising in ways that early-detection and diagnostic-system investments have not yet offset, particularly for metastatic disease and in LMICs. The JCO Korean cohort adds rare high-quality evidence that modifiable exposures (alcohol) drive early-onset solid tumours, reinforcing prevention as a complementary strategy.
