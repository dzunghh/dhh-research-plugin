## Appraisal notes

The five papers share a common thread of rigour about what we know and don't know in cancer epidemiology. The NordICC 13-year update (Lancet) is the most immediately policy-relevant: single-offer colonoscopy demonstrably reduces CRC incidence but its failure to show a significant mortality benefit — driven partly by lower-than-expected background mortality — challenges the primacy of colonoscopy in screening programmes and echoes the COLONPREV FIT-vs-colonoscopy literature. The Ward microsimulation (Lancet Oncology) and the NPC host–virus interaction paper (Nature) together illustrate two ends of the causal inference spectrum: the former quantifying the macroscopic burden gap in LMICs, the latter resolving a long-standing aetiological question in viral oncology with mechanistic precision. The Maas epigenetics paper adds picloram herbicide to the short list of environmental candidates for early-onset CRC, while Sanderson et al. provide a timely methods corrective for the field as MR proliferates.
