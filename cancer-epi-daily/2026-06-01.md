## 1. Daraxonrasib or Chemotherapy in Previously Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: N Engl J Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: O'Reilly EM et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate oral RAS(ON) multi-selective inhibitor daraxonrasib vs chemotherapy in previously treated metastatic PDAC with KRAS mutations.
- **Method**: Phase 3, open-label, international RCT; n=500; dual primary endpoints OS and PFS in KRAS G12 population.
- **Key findings**: Median OS 13.2 vs 6.6 months (HR 0.40, P<0.001); median PFS 7.3 vs 3.5 months (HR 0.45, P<0.001) in KRAS G12 population.
- **Strengths**: Large international Phase 3 RCT; >90% KRAS G12 prevalence; robust dual-primary design with prespecified subgroups.
- **Limitations**: Open-label design; OS interim analysis; crossover not permitted from chemo to daraxonrasib.
- **Conclusion**: Daraxonrasib provides clinically meaningful OS and PFS benefit over chemotherapy in previously treated KRAS-mutant metastatic PDAC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMoa2605555 / PMID:42223072

## 2. PARP and Androgen-Signaling Inhibition plus ADT in Metastatic Prostate Cancer (TALAPRO-3) — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: N Engl J Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-30
- **Authors**: Agarwal N et al.
- **Aims**: Assess talazoparib plus enzalutamide vs placebo plus enzalutamide in metastatic androgen pathway modulation-sensitive prostate cancer with HRR gene alterations.
- **Method**: Phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT; n=599; primary endpoint imaging-based progression-free survival; stratified by disease status, volume, and BRCA mutation status.
- **Key findings**: 3-year PFS 77% vs 56% (HR 0.48, P<0.001); interim OS 78% vs 72% (HR 0.77, 95% CI 0.56–1.04).
- **Strengths**: Double-blind Phase 3 design; biomarker-selected HRR population; extended 3-year follow-up reported.
- **Limitations**: OS data immature at interim; higher grade ≥3 anemia (51%) in talazoparib arm; two treatment-related deaths.
- **Conclusion**: Talazoparib plus enzalutamide significantly improves PFS vs enzalutamide alone in HRR-altered metastatic hormone-sensitive prostate cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMoa2604126 / PMID:42223064

## 3. Perioperative Apalutamide in High-Risk Localized Prostate Cancer (PROTEUS) — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: N Engl J Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: Taplin ME et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate perioperative ADT plus apalutamide vs ADT plus placebo on pathological response and metastasis-free survival in high-risk localized/locally advanced prostate cancer.
- **Method**: Phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT; n=2109; dual primary endpoints pathological complete response/minimal residual disease and metastasis-free survival; median follow-up 61.7 months.
- **Key findings**: Pathological complete response/minimal residual disease 8.9% vs 1.0% (OR 10.17, P<0.001); 5-year MFS probability 78.2% vs 73.5% (HR 0.80, P=0.02).
- **Strengths**: Largest perioperative prostate cancer RCT to date; dual primary endpoint design; >5-year follow-up.
- **Limitations**: More grade 3–4 adverse events with apalutamide (39.6% vs 31.0%); pathological CR rate modest in absolute terms.
- **Conclusion**: Perioperative apalutamide significantly improves pathological response and metastasis-free survival in high-risk localized prostate cancer undergoing radical prostatectomy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMoa2603878 / PMID:42223077

## 4. Multivariate Mendelian Randomization for Joint Inferences of Correlated Outcomes — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Eur J Epidemiol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-28
- **Authors**: Zhang Y et al.
- **Aims**: Develop multivariate MR methods that jointly analyze multiple correlated outcomes to improve causal inference efficiency.
- **Method**: Two novel methods — multivariate MR-IVW (via multivariate meta-analysis incorporating outcome correlations) and multivariate MR-PRESSO (Mahalanobis distance-based pleiotropy detection); validated by simulation and Framingham/GTEx real-data application.
- **Key findings**: Multivariate MR-IVW achieved 95% vs 52% sensitivity for global testing at r=0.8; multivariate MR-PRESSO detected 85–90% of outlying SNPs vs 35–40% with univariate.
- **Strengths**: Theoretically principled framework; comprehensive simulation across correlation structures; open real-data validation using established cohorts.
- **Limitations**: Assumes shared instrument validity across outcomes; real-data example uses non-cancer outcomes; no software package described in abstract.
- **Conclusion**: Multivariate MR methods provide substantially superior power and pleiotropy detection when outcomes are correlated, enabling rigorous multi-omics causal inference.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1007/s10654-026-01406-1 / PMID:42207415

## 5. Selpercatinib in Early-Stage RET Fusion-Positive Non-Small-Cell Lung Cancer (LIBRETTO-432) — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: N Engl J Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-31
- **Authors**: Wu YL et al.
- **Aims**: Assess adjuvant selpercatinib vs placebo after definitive therapy in early-stage RET fusion-positive NSCLC.
- **Method**: Phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT; n=151; primary endpoint investigator-assessed event-free survival in stage II–IIIA; median follow-up 24–27 months.
- **Key findings**: 2-year EFS 92% vs 61% in stage II–IIIA (HR 0.17, P<0.001); consistent results in stage IB–IIIA (94% vs 70%, HR 0.17, P<0.001).
- **Strengths**: Highly significant EFS benefit; precision oncology approach; blinded independent central review confirms investigator assessment.
- **Limitations**: Small n=151 due to RET rarity (~1–2% of NSCLC); OS data immature; grade ≥3 transaminase elevations in 17–19%.
- **Conclusion**: Adjuvant selpercatinib dramatically improves event-free survival in early-stage RET fusion-positive NSCLC, establishing a new standard of care in this biomarker-defined population.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMoa2602628 / PMID:42223087

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest is dominated by high-impact Phase 3 RCTs reflecting two converging trends: precision oncology biomarker stratification (HRR alterations in prostate cancer, KRAS G12 in pancreatic cancer, RET fusions in NSCLC) and perioperative intensification of systemic therapy before/after curative surgery. The RAS inhibitor daraxonrasib result for pancreatic cancer (OS HR 0.40) is particularly striking given the historically dismal prognosis of this disease. On the methodological side, the multivariate Mendelian randomization paper from Eur J Epidemiol addresses a genuine gap — most MR studies treat correlated outcomes independently — and the demonstrated power gains at high inter-outcome correlations (r=0.8: 95% vs 52% sensitivity) have broad implications for multi-trait cancer-risk studies.
