## 1. Cervical cancer mortality trends following HPV vaccination in England, 2001–24 — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Lancet
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-17
- **Authors**: Sasieni P et al.
- **Aims**: Quantify cervical cancer mortality reductions associated with the England national HPV vaccination programme introduced in 2008.
- **Method**: Population-based mortality analysis of women aged 20–34, England 2001–2024; Poisson regression with vaccination-coverage adjustment; herd immunity excluded (conservative).
- **Key findings**: 100% mortality reduction (95% CI 84–100) in women aged 20–24 with ~88–90% coverage; ~200 cervical cancer deaths averted by end of 2024.
- **Strengths**: 24-year national mortality time series; birth-cohort coverage linkage; first national-level evidence of HPV-vaccine impact on cancer mortality.
- **Limitations**: Observational study; cervical screening contributions not fully disentangled; herd immunity excluded (likely underestimates effect).
- **Conclusion**: High-coverage HPV vaccination is associated with near-elimination of cervical cancer deaths in young women, supporting WHO elimination goals.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S0140-6736(26)00918-9 / PMID:42309117

## 2. ARTIMES: AI-assisted volumetric response criteria in pleural mesothelioma — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-17
- **Authors**: Lipman KBWG et al.
- **Aims**: Develop and validate AI-assisted volumetric response criteria for pleural mesothelioma to overcome limitations of diameter-based mRECIST.
- **Method**: Retrospective, multicohort, multicentre study; deep-learning segmentation trained on 10,926 CT scans (2,080 patients, 14 cohorts); validated in 8 clinical trials (4,674 CT scans, 943 patients).
- **Key findings**: ARTIMES superior prognostic concordance vs mRECIST (c-index 0.83 vs 0.73; p=0.023); detected progression median 5 weeks earlier (124 vs 162 days).
- **Strengths**: Unprecedented dataset scale; biologically derived thresholds; trial-level surrogate endpoint validation against overall survival.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective design; prospective validation pending; external DSC only 71–80% with manual segmentations.
- **Conclusion**: ARTIMES improves response evaluation and shows stronger overall-survival surrogacy than mRECIST, potentially enabling more reliable mesothelioma trial endpoints.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00084-7 / PMID:42309108

## 3. HARMONi-A RCT: Ivonescimab plus chemotherapy in EGFR-variant NSCLC — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-17
- **Authors**: Fang W et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether ivonescimab (PD-1 × VEGF bispecific antibody) added to chemotherapy improves overall survival in EGFR-variant NSCLC after EGFR-TKI failure.
- **Method**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial; 55 sites, China; 322 patients; median follow-up 32.5 months; hierarchical endpoint testing.
- **Key findings**: Ivonescimab + chemo improved OS (16.8 vs 14.1 months; HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.58–0.95; P=0.02); 30-month survival 29.1% vs 18.4%.
- **Strengths**: Phase 3 RCT with mature OS data; double-blind design; biologically novel dual immune-angiogenic targeting.
- **Limitations**: China-only cohort limits generalizability; grade ≥3 AEs higher with ivonescimab (67.1% vs 54.7%).
- **Conclusion**: Ivonescimab plus chemotherapy provides a statistically significant and clinically meaningful OS benefit in EGFR-TKI-resistant NSCLC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jama.2026.7745 / PMID:42307937

## 4. Single-fraction SBRT for localized prostate cancer: phase 1/2 trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-18
- **Authors**: Zilli T et al.
- **Aims**: Assess whether single-fraction 19-Gy urethra-sparing SBRT achieves biochemical disease control with acceptable toxicity in low/intermediate-risk prostate cancer.
- **Method**: Multicenter, single-arm, prospective phase 1/2 trial; 43 patients treated per protocol; 5 academic centres (Europe and US); median follow-up 55.3 months.
- **Key findings**: 3-year biochemical relapse-free survival 92.9% (95% CI 85.4–100%), meeting primary endpoint; grade 2 GU/GI AEs <10%/<5% at 3 years.
- **Strengths**: Multinational design; long follow-up (>4 years); urethra-sparing technique; prospective QOL assessment.
- **Limitations**: Single arm, no comparator; small sample (n=43); 3 years insufficient for definitive prostate cancer endpoint assessment.
- **Conclusion**: Single 19-Gy SBRT fraction meets biochemical efficacy threshold with acceptable toxicity; phase 3 confirmation warranted.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1886 / PMID:42313395

## 5. Management of Differentiated Thyroid Cancer — Score 3/6

- **Journal**: New England Journal of Medicine
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-18
- **Authors**: Hegedüs L et al.
- **Aims**: Present a risk-adapted clinical decision-making framework for differentiated thyroid cancer from nodule detection through advanced disease.
- **Method**: Comprehensive narrative review integrating clinicopathological staging, molecular risk characterisation, and patient preference; covers active surveillance through systemic therapies.
- **Key findings**: Active surveillance is appropriate for low-risk papillary microcarcinoma; targeted therapies improving advanced disease outcomes; risk stratification must be dynamic throughout care.
- **Strengths**: NEJM-level synthesis of heterogeneous evidence; explicit patient-preference integration; full management spectrum covered.
- **Limitations**: Narrative review without systematic methodology; heterogeneous underlying evidence base; overdiagnosis implications not fully quantified.
- **Conclusion**: Dynamic molecular and clinicopathological risk stratification enables personalized thyroid cancer management that avoids over-treatment without compromising disease control.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMra2416814 / PMID:42308485
