## 1. Plasma proteomic signatures of cellular aging predict human disease — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nat Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-15
- **Authors**: Ding DY et al.
- **Aims**: Test whether plasma proteomics can quantify cell-type-specific biological aging and predict incident disease prospectively.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort (N=60,542); 7,000+ plasma proteins; ML models estimating biological age of 40+ cell types; 15-year disease follow-up.
- **Key findings**: Extreme respiratory epithelial aging associated with 58% higher lung cancer risk beyond smoking alone; extreme skeletal myocyte aging linked to 12.7-fold ALS risk.
- **Strengths**: Exceptionally large population (N=60,542), 15-year follow-up, multi-cell-type resolution, validated across cohorts and proteomics platforms.
- **Limitations**: Cell-type aging inferred from plasma proteins via ML; causal directionality not established.
- **Conclusion**: Plasma proteomics-derived cellular aging profiles stratify cancer and mortality risk beyond established clinical predictors.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41591-026-04446-y / PMID:42297981

## 2. The Landscape of Prostate Tumour Methylation — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Discov
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-17
- **Authors**: Arbet J et al.
- **Aims**: Characterise the epigenomic landscape of prostate cancer across ancestries and disease stages from normal tissue to poly-metastatic disease.
- **Method**: Compendium of 3,001 multi-ancestry prostate methylomes; 884 samples with multi-omic DNA/RNA characterisation; unsupervised epigenomic subtyping.
- **Key findings**: Four epigenomic subtypes identified that risk-stratify patients; epigenetic signatures predict 15 clinico-molecular features including patient outcome, synergistic with clinical prognostic tools.
- **Strengths**: Largest prostate methylome compendium to date; multi-ancestry design; multi-omic integration with clinical annotation.
- **Limitations**: Observational cross-sectional design; causal mechanisms of epigenomic subtype transitions not yet established.
- **Conclusion**: Epigenomic subtyping provides prognostic information complementary to standard clinical staging in prostate cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/2159-8290.CD-25-0761 / PMID:42307031
