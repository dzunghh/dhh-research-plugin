## 1. Mapping DNA Glycosylase Binding Across Lesion Sequence Contexts Reveals Extended Sequence and Structural Recognition Logic — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-06
- **Authors**: Levy N et al.
- **Aims**: To characterize how DNA glycosylases recognize cytosine-deamination lesions across thousands of sequence contexts and link repair specificity to cancer mutational signatures.
- **Method**: High-throughput binding assay quantifying UDG, TDG, and MBD4 binding across thousands of lesion-containing sequences, combined with structural analysis and molecular dynamics simulations.
- **Key findings**: Glycosylase binding depends strongly on sequence context extending several bases beyond the lesion, with non-additive neighbour interactions. Nearest-neighbour preferences resemble deamination-related cancer mutational signatures.
- **Strengths**: High-throughput generalizable platform, integrated structural and population genomic validation.
- **Limitations**: In vitro binding assay; cellular context and competing repair pathways not assessed.
- **Conclusion**: Repair specificity of DNA glycosylases directly shapes cancer mutational signature landscapes, providing a mechanistic epi bridge.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74090-0 / PMID:42251035

## 2. Targeting Fibroblast TXNDC5 Resolves Tumor Desmoplasia and PD-1 Resistance in Colorectal Cancer with Mesenchymal Traits — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-05
- **Authors**: Cheng KL et al.
- **Aims**: To determine whether fibroblast TXNDC5 drives the immunosuppressive niche and PD-1 resistance in mesenchymal-type colorectal cancer.
- **Method**: Fibroblast-specific Txndc5 knockout mouse models combined with human CRC tumor profiling; mechanistic interrogation of the TXNDC5–TGFBR1 axis.
- **Key findings**: TXNDC5 deletion attenuated tumor stiffness, reduced hypoxia, boosted cytotoxic T-cell recruitment, and markedly sensitised tumors to PD-1 blockade. TXNDC5 stabilises TGFBR1 to drive fibroblast activation.
- **Strengths**: Genetic loss-of-function model plus mechanistic pathway elucidation with translational patient data.
- **Limitations**: Mouse model; mesenchymal CRC subtype selection may limit generalisability.
- **Conclusion**: TXNDC5–TGFBR1 axis is a targetable stromal driver of CRC immunotherapy resistance.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74073-1 / PMID:42248857

## 3. HMGCS1 Drives Cholesterol-Dependent Membrane Repair and Shields Tumor Cells from Lymphocyte Attack — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-05
- **Authors**: Zhang Y et al.
- **Aims**: To identify metabolic enzymes that enable tumor cells to evade cytotoxic lymphocyte killing via plasma-membrane repair.
- **Method**: Functional screen of 111 metabolic enzymes; HMGCS1 validated in NK, CAR-T, and anti-PD-1 models; patient lung cancer IHC and clinical outcome correlation.
- **Key findings**: HMGCS1-driven de novo cholesterol synthesis repairs perforin-induced membrane pores; elevated c-Jun/HMGCS1/cholesterol in lung cancer patients correlated with reduced anti-PD-1 efficacy.
- **Strengths**: Broad screen, multiple immunotherapy modalities tested, patient correlate data.
- **Limitations**: Patient correlation is observational IHC; causality in humans unproven.
- **Conclusion**: HMGCS1-mediated membrane repair is a tumour-intrinsic immune-evasion mechanism actionable for combination immunotherapy.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74022-y / PMID:42248910

## 4. IQGAP3 Bridges Matrix Stiffness with Glioma Stem Cell Maintenance and Radioresistance by Stabilizing SOX2 — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-06
- **Authors**: Zhang P et al.
- **Aims**: To identify how tumour microenvironment stiffness promotes glioblastoma radioresistance and maintain stem-cell hierarchies.
- **Method**: Differential gene expression in GBM stem cells on stiff matrices; IQGAP3 knockdown in vitro and in vivo; FDA-approved drug screen for IQGAP3–SOX2 disruptors.
- **Key findings**: Stiff matrices induce IQGAP3 via YAP1/TEAD, stabilising SOX2 and conferring radioresistance; trimetrexate identified as brain-penetrant IQGAP3 inhibitor sensitising GBM to radiotherapy.
- **Strengths**: Mechanistic clarity from in vitro to in vivo; actionable FDA-approved drug identified.
- **Limitations**: Mouse orthotopic model; clinical translation for trimetrexate needs prospective testing.
- **Conclusion**: IQGAP3–SOX2 is a biomechanically driven radioresistance axis with an immediately testable pharmacological solution.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-74058-0 / PMID:42251046

## 5. Circulating N6-Methyladenosine RNA as a Diagnostic and Stage-Associated Prognostic Biomarker in Colorectal Cancer — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: BMC Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-15
- **Authors**: Lin YT et al.
- **Aims**: To evaluate serum extracellular-vesicle m6A-modified RNA as a liquid biopsy biomarker for CRC detection and prognosis.
- **Method**: Cross-sectional biomarker study; EV-m6A RNA quantified by ELISA in 76 CRC patients and 30 healthy controls; ROC and survival analyses.
- **Key findings**: EV-m6A RNA distinguished CRC from controls with AUC 0.83 (95% CI 0.76–0.91); late-stage CRC had higher levels than early-stage (p<0.001); elevated levels predicted worse OS (HR 3.94, p=0.011).
- **Strengths**: Simultaneous diagnostic and prognostic validation in same cohort; non-invasive liquid biopsy approach.
- **Limitations**: Single-centre, small sample (n=76); m6A specificity vs. global RNA modification changes needs clarification.
- **Conclusion**: Circulating EV-m6A RNA is a feasible non-invasive biomarker for CRC diagnosis and staging, warranting larger-scale validation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12885-026-16290-x / PMID:42231202
