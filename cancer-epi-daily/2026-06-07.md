## 1. Change in Body Mass Index and Physical Activity and Postmenopausal Breast Cancer Risk in a Cohort of Dutch Nurses — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Journal of the National Cancer Institute
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-05
- **Authors**: van Duijne HM et al.
- **Aims**: Examine how lifetime changes in BMI and physical activity from age 18 to menopause affect postmenopausal breast cancer risk.
- **Method**: Prospective cohort (43,127 Dutch nurses, 1,776 incident breast cancers), multivariable Cox regression with repeated BMI/PA measures over three time points.
- **Key findings**: Each 5 kg weight gain from age 18 to menopause increased risk HR=1.06 (95%CI 1.04–1.09); developing overweight/obesity by menopause HR=1.39 (95%CI 1.06–1.83).
- **Strengths**: Large cohort with longitudinal repeated exposure assessment spanning decades; precise timing relative to menopause.
- **Limitations**: Nurses cohort limits generalizability; physical activity self-reported and may be subject to recall bias.
- **Conclusion**: Weight gain from young adulthood to menopause independently elevates postmenopausal breast cancer risk regardless of physical activity level.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1093/jnci/djag177 / PMID:42249545

## 2. A Saliva-Based Surrogate Associates with Clinical Outcome of Oral Potentially Malignant Disorders — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: British Journal of Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-06
- **Authors**: Chen YW et al.
- **Aims**: Develop and validate non-invasive salivary cytokine biomarkers to predict malignant transformation of oral potentially malignant disorders (OPMDs).
- **Method**: Prospective multicenter cohort (TWOPMD; 8 Taiwan health centers, 1,313 saliva samples); Cox regression on 749 longitudinally followed OPMD patients; RNA-seq discovery in 40 OPMDs.
- **Key findings**: IL-6/IL-1Ra ratio was the strongest independent predictor (p=0.00001); adding betel nut status further improved prognostic performance.
- **Strengths**: Large multicenter prospective design, non-invasive sample, simultaneous discovery and validation, clinical risk factor integration.
- **Limitations**: Taiwan-specific exposure profile (betel nut); RNA-seq discovery cohort small (n=40); external geographic validation not yet performed.
- **Conclusion**: Salivary cytokine ratios provide a clinically actionable, non-invasive risk stratification tool for oral cancer surveillance.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03486-y / PMID:42251141

## 3. Multimodal Artificial Intelligence Prediction of Abiraterone Efficacy in Two STAMPEDE Phase 3 Trials of Non-Metastatic Very High-Risk Prostate Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Annals of Oncology
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-05
- **Authors**: Parker CTA et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether a locked multimodal AI (MMAI) model can predict which patients benefit from adding abiraterone to long-term ADT in very high-risk localized prostate cancer.
- **Method**: Post-hoc analysis of two sequential STAMPEDE RCTs (n=1,137); MMAI integrates digital pathology, PSA, tumor stage, and age; Cox regression with treatment-by-biomarker interaction test.
- **Key findings**: MMAI very high-risk group achieved 5-year MFS of 81% vs 62% with abiraterone (HR=0.47, 95%CI 0.31–0.70); standard-risk showed no benefit (HR=0.83; interaction p=0.02).
- **Strengths**: Two independent RCT datasets, pre-specified threshold, large trial population, locked biomarker model avoiding overfitting.
- **Limitations**: Post-hoc design; MMAI developed by industry (Artera); interaction p=0.02 is marginal.
- **Conclusion**: Multimodal AI enables precision selection of patients most likely to benefit from treatment intensification in high-risk prostate cancer.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.708 / PMID:42250604

## 4. Leveraging Commercially Available Protein Assays as Biomarkers for Lung Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-03
- **Authors**: McGann KC et al.
- **Aims**: Assess whether four commercial blood protein assays (CYFRA 21-1, CEA, CA-125, HE-4) can improve lung cancer diagnosis in patients with indeterminate pulmonary nodules (IPNs).
- **Method**: Prospective collection, retrospective blinded evaluation; train (n=371), test (n=166), and external validation (LTP-2 multicenter, n=279) cohorts; logistic regression with AUC.
- **Key findings**: Combined 4-biomarker model AUC=0.70 (0.65–0.74) in Train+Test; validated at AUC=0.61 (0.54–0.68) in LTP-2; CA-125 failed to validate (LTP-2 AUC=0.48).
- **Strengths**: External multicenter validation in LTP-2; fully commercial assays enable rapid clinical translation.
- **Limitations**: Moderate AUC in external validation; CA-125 non-reproducible; single-institution training cohort.
- **Conclusion**: Commercially available protein assays add diagnostic value for lung cancer risk stratification and could be quickly integrated into IPN evaluation workflows.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-2004 / PMID:42233870

## 5. Impacts of a Public Health Emergency on Cancer Testing Rates, Follow-up, and Cancer Diagnosis: an Evaluation of the COVID-19 Pandemic — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiology, Biomarkers & Prevention
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-03
- **Authors**: Del Vecchio NJ et al.
- **Aims**: Quantify COVID-19's impact on cervical, colorectal, and lung cancer screening rates, 6-month follow-up of abnormal results, and cancer incidence across 10 US health systems.
- **Method**: Population-based multi-health system analysis (PROSPR consortium, n=10 sites); monthly screening rates, 6-month follow-up proportions, and cancer incidence rates compared pre-COVID vs COVID periods.
- **Key findings**: Testing declined 18–96% in the first two pandemic months; 6-month follow-up rates trended downward; systems using mailed FIT kits with uninterrupted outreach showed the smallest disruptions.
- **Strengths**: 10 diverse US health systems, three cancer types, PROSPR consortium infrastructure, embedded healthcare system surveys.
- **Limitations**: Residual confounding by unmeasured system factors; limited ability to assess downstream late-stage diagnosis.
- **Conclusion**: Remote screening outreach (e.g., mailed FIT) that continued uninterrupted through a public health emergency best preserved cancer screening continuity.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0292 / PMID:42233964
