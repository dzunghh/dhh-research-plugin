## 1. Chronic stress unleashes an intratumor phage-fibroblast-B cell circuit to promote tumor growth — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Cell
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Bashir H et al.
- **Aims**: Investigate how chronic stress-induced gut microbiota perturbations impair anti-tumour germinal centre B cell responses.
- **Method**: Mouse models of colorectal cancer and melanoma; mechanistic dissection of gut pathobiont Enterococcus gallinarum (Eg) translocation plus human tumour validation.
- **Key findings**: Chronic stress drives Eg translocation to tumours; intratumoral phage DNA activates TLR9 in CAFs to produce glucocorticoids, suppressing B cell anti-tumour responses. Targeting TLR9 or Eg reversed stress-induced tumour promotion.
- **Strengths**: Multi-model validation (mouse CRC, melanoma) combined with mechanistic dissection and human tumour data provides strong triangulation.
- **Limitations**: Causal pathway not yet confirmed in prospective human cohorts; complex mouse-to-human translation.
- **Conclusion**: Reveals a stress→gut dysbiosis→CAF→B cell immunosuppression axis as a targetable driver of tumour progression.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.ccell.2026.06.004 / PMID:42349430

## 2. Long-Term Follow-Up of JCOG1008, a Randomized Phase II/III Trial of Chemoradiotherapy Comparing 3-Weekly Cisplatin With Weekly Cisplatin in Postoperative Head and Neck Cancer — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-26
- **Authors**: Tahara M et al.
- **Aims**: Confirm non-inferiority of weekly versus 3-weekly cisplatin chemoradiotherapy in postoperative high-risk locally advanced squamous cell carcinoma of the head and neck (LA-SCCHN).
- **Method**: Randomised phase II/III trial; 261 patients; median follow-up 5.6 years; non-inferiority design.
- **Key findings**: 5-year OS 71.2% (weekly) vs 58.7% (3-weekly), HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.52–1.12), confirming non-inferiority. No clinically meaningful difference in late toxicity between arms.
- **Strengths**: Long-term follow-up (5.6 years), multi-centre randomised design, confirms interim non-inferiority with mature survival data.
- **Limitations**: Single-country (Japan) cohort limits generalisability; non-inferiority margin of 1.32 may be debated in international practice.
- **Conclusion**: Weekly cisplatin plus radiotherapy is confirmed non-inferior to 3-weekly for postoperative high-risk LA-SCCHN and is a viable standard-of-care alternative.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-01708 / PMID:42361282
