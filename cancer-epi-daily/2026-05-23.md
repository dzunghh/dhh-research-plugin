## 1. Integrating In Situ Immune Expression Landscape with Gene Expression-Based Subtyping to Characterize Breast Cancer Heterogeneity — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Abubakar M et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate whether an 8-marker IHC panel can capture immune heterogeneity across intrinsic breast cancer subtypes as effectively as RNA-seq-based subtyping.
- **Method**: Cross-sectional study (n=148 invasive breast cancer); dual IHC for CD3, CD8, CD20, FOXP3, CD68, CD163, PDL1, IDO1 with PanCK; digital image analysis; compared against PAM50 and RNA-seq immune subtypes.
- **Key findings**: IHC markers strongly correlated with gene expression immune subtypes; high-TIL tumors showed elevated CD3, CD8, CD20, PDL1, IDO1; high-ISG tumors additionally distinguished by elevated CD163.
- **Strengths**: Parsimonious IHC panel validated against RNA-seq; directly relevant to resource-limited clinical settings.
- **Limitations**: Single cohort (Hong Kong), n=148; cross-sectional design limits causal inference.
- **Conclusion**: IHC-based immune classification can approximate gene expression subtypes, supporting broader clinical adoption for breast cancer stratification.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-25-2068 / PMID:42171349

## 2. A Scalable Tn5-Based Method for Genome-Wide DNA Methylation Profiling in Development and Disease — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nat Commun
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Hu H et al.
- **Aims**: Develop a low-input, low-cost alternative to bisulfite sequencing for genome-wide DNA methylation profiling applicable to cancer biomarker classification.
- **Method**: CCUT&Tag uses methylation-binding domain fusion proteins with Tn5 transposase for selective adapter insertion into methylated DNA; validated across human stem cells, organoids, zebrafish embryos, and tumor biopsies; concordance tested against bisulfite sequencing; applied to brain tumor subtype classification.
- **Key findings**: Strong concordance with bisulfite sequencing at markedly lower sequencing depth; correctly classified brain tumor methylation subtypes from biopsies.
- **Strengths**: Scalable, cost-effective, low-input requirements; validated across multiple tissue systems including tumors.
- **Limitations**: Validation cohort of tumor biopsies is small; not yet tested in large prospective cancer-epi cohorts.
- **Conclusion**: CCUT&Tag enables practical genome-wide methylation profiling for cancer subtype classification and epidemiologic biomarker research.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73325-4 / PMID:42173849

## 3. Effects of Ambient PM2.5 and Its Components on the Prognosis of Colorectal Cancer Survivors: Disease Progression and Potential Mediation Pathways — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: BMC Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Qin Q et al.
- **Aims**: Examine whether long-term PM2.5 exposure affects mortality and disease progression transitions in non-metastatic colorectal cancer survivors, and identify inflammatory mediators.
- **Method**: Retrospective cohort (n=3,357 CRC patients, 2012–2017); annual PM2.5 from CHAP satellite dataset; Cox proportional hazards models; multi-state models for disease transitions; mediation analysis for systemic inflammation and oxidative stress biomarkers.
- **Key findings**: Each IQR increase in PM2.5 associated with HR=1.35 (95% CI 1.18–1.54) for CRC mortality; sulfate component showed HR=1.68; MLR mediated 8.9% of the effect.
- **Strengths**: Large cohort; novel multi-state framework captures prognosis at each disease transition; component-specific PM2.5 analysis.
- **Limitations**: Single-center retrospective design; residential address used as proxy for exposure; residual confounding possible.
- **Conclusion**: Long-term PM2.5 exposure worsens CRC prognosis via systemic inflammation and oxidative stress, supporting integrated survivorship and air quality policies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s12916-026-04939-0 / PMID:42174596

## 4. Longitudinal Transcriptomic Profiling Identifies Predictors of Response to Neoadjuvant Chemoimmunotherapy in Triple-Negative Breast Cancer: Results from the NeoTRIPaPDL1 Trial — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Ann Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-19
- **Authors**: Dugo M et al.
- **Aims**: Identify predictive biomarkers for pathologic complete response (pCR) to neoadjuvant chemoimmunotherapy in high-risk TNBC using longitudinal tumor sampling.
- **Method**: Biomarker sub-study of phase III NeoTRIPaPDL1 RCT (n=280); baseline and on-treatment (D1C2) RNA-seq; hallmark, immune, ferroptosis-related gene signatures; univariable logistic regression and multivariable XGBoost models.
- **Key findings**: Absence of tumor cells in early on-treatment biopsies was the strongest pCR predictor in both arms; immune programs became dominant correlates on-treatment, superseding baseline metabolic signatures.
- **Strengths**: RCT design with longitudinal sampling; multivariable XGBoost captures complementary biomarker contributions.
- **Limitations**: Single trial; specific carboplatin/nab-paclitaxel/atezolizumab regimen; external validation cohort needed.
- **Conclusion**: Early tumor clearance and on-treatment immune activation are complementary pCR predictors, supporting response-adapted neoadjuvant strategies in TNBC.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.05.694 / PMID:42162664

## 5. Near-Infrared Fluorescence Imaging-Guided Surgery Using cRGD-ZW800 to Improve Surgical Resection Margins in Oral Cancer: A Phase I/II Feasibility Trial — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nat Commun
- **Pub date**: 2026-05-22
- **Authors**: Zweedijk BE et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety and feasibility of cRGD-ZW800-1, a near-infrared integrin-targeted tracer, for intraoperative margin detection in oral squamous cell carcinoma.
- **Method**: Prospective single-center Phase I/II trial (n=31; NCT04191460); three dose levels (0.01, 0.025, 0.05 mg/kg); in vivo spectroscopy to optimize dosing; fluorescence vs. conventional margin assessment compared.
- **Key findings**: Optimal dose 0.025 mg/kg; tumor-to-background ratio >4.5; fluorescence detected all 23 inadequate margins (sensitivity 100%) vs. 70% by conventional assessment; altered surgical plans in 5 cases.
- **Strengths**: Prospective design; quantitative spectroscopy for dosing; head-to-head comparison with standard-of-care margin assessment.
- **Limitations**: Small single-center study (n=31); feasibility phase only; survival outcomes not yet reported.
- **Conclusion**: cRGD-ZW800-1 is safe and significantly improves intraoperative margin detection in oral cancer, warranting larger confirmatory trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73554-7 / PMID:42173866
