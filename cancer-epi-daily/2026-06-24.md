## 1. A multi-state survival model to identify risk factors for lethal ovarian cancer — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Cancer Epidemiol Biomarkers Prev
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-24
- **Authors**: Townsend MK et al.
- **Aims**: Identify pre-diagnosis risk factors jointly predicting ovarian cancer incidence and disease-specific mortality in cancer-free women.
- **Method**: Multi-state survival model across three 12-year periods (1980–2017) in Nurses' Health Study and NHSII; N=211,420 cancer-free women, 1,730 incident ovarian cancers, 660 deaths.
- **Key findings**: Nulliparity amplified lethal ovarian cancer risk (RR 1.62, 95% CI 1.23–2.13); oral contraceptive use ≥10 years was protective (RR 0.65, 95% CI 0.43–0.97); ≥20 pack-years smoking elevated risk (RR 1.25, 95% CI 1.02–1.53).
- **Strengths**: Very large established cohort; multi-state framework captures differential exposure effects on incidence vs. mortality simultaneously.
- **Limitations**: NHS cohort is predominantly white nurses, limiting generalisability; exposures are self-reported.
- **Conclusion**: Multi-state modelling reveals exposures with distinct effects on ovarian cancer development vs. death, improving risk stratification for targeted prevention.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1158/1055-9965.EPI-26-0152 / PMID:42340350

## 2. Ten-Year Outcomes after CAR T-Cell Therapy for B-Cell Lymphomas — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: N Engl J Med
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-25
- **Authors**: Ruella M et al.
- **Aims**: Establish decade-long outcomes and curative potential of tisagenlecleucel (CTL019) CAR T-cell therapy in relapsed/refractory B-cell lymphomas.
- **Method**: Long-term observational follow-up of 38 patients (original CTL019 trial; 24 large B-cell, 14 follicular); median follow-up 10.1 years; Aalen-Johansen competing risks method.
- **Key findings**: 10-year lymphoma-free survival was 32% (large B-cell) and 47% (follicular); no relapses beyond 5.4 years; second primary cancer developed in 9 patients (cumulative incidence 21% at 10 years).
- **Strengths**: Longest follow-up on any approved CAR T product; competing risks approach for non-relapse mortality and second cancers; confirms curative potential.
- **Limitations**: Small cohort (N=38), single-arm, single institution, not directly comparable to modern CAR T products.
- **Conclusion**: A single tisagenlecleucel infusion produces decade-long remissions in ~32–47% of heavily pre-treated B-cell lymphoma patients, establishing clinically meaningful curative potential.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1056/NEJMoa2518035 / PMID:42341302

## 3. Comprehensive Smoke-Free Laws and Cardiovascular Disease Mortality in US Counties — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Netw Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01 (EDAT: 2026-06-24)
- **Authors**: Wu C et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate long-term association between county-level comprehensive 100% smoke-free laws and CVD mortality, with subgroup analyses by age, sex, and race/ethnicity.
- **Method**: Comparative effectiveness study using generalised synthetic control method; 38 treated vs. 103 control US counties (2000–2018); staggered policy adoption; parametric bootstrapping for 95% CIs.
- **Key findings**: Smoke-free laws associated with mean annual reduction of 12.0 CVD deaths per 100,000 (95% CI −21.3 to −2.7) over 12 post-policy years; larger reductions in adults ≥65 years and males; smaller and imprecise in non-Hispanic Black populations.
- **Strengths**: Rigorous quasi-experimental design (generalised synthetic control) mitigates confounding in staggered policy adoption; 18-year longitudinal panel; relevant epi methodology for cancer policy research.
- **Limitations**: County-level ecological study (no individual data); CVD rather than cancer as primary outcome; residual confounding from unmeasured time-varying factors.
- **Conclusion**: Comprehensive smoke-free laws produce sustained county-level CVD mortality reductions over 12 years, with demographic heterogeneity highlighting need for supplemental tobacco control strategies.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.21170 / PMID:42340981

## 4. SACI-IO HR+: A Randomized Phase II Trial of Sacituzumab Govitecan ± Pembrolizumab in Metastatic HR+/HER2− Breast Cancer — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Ann Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-20
- **Authors**: Garrido-Castro AC et al.
- **Aims**: Determine whether adding pembrolizumab (PD-1 inhibitor) to sacituzumab govitecan (TROP2-directed ADC) improves outcomes in HR+/HER2− metastatic breast cancer.
- **Method**: Randomised phase II trial (1:1), N=104, primary endpoint PFS; 15.5-month median follow-up; correlative plasma epigenome, ctDNA, and PIK3CA mutation analyses.
- **Key findings**: SG+pembrolizumab did not significantly improve PFS vs. SG alone (8.4 vs. 6.7 months; HR 0.76, p=0.12); in PD-L1+ subgroup (44%), PFS trend favoured combination (11.1 vs. 5.6 months, p=0.09).
- **Strengths**: Randomised design with comprehensive exploratory biomarker programme (ctDNA fraction, PIK3CA, plasma epigenome-pathway); multi-centre US trial.
- **Limitations**: Phase II, underpowered for subgroup analyses; primary endpoint not met; PD-L1+ finding requires larger randomised confirmation.
- **Conclusion**: Pembrolizumab addition to sacituzumab govitecan was not superior in unselected HR+/HER2− MBC; PD-L1+ enrichment may identify a responsive subgroup warranting phase III evaluation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1016/j.annonc.2026.06.012 / PMID:42323161

## 5. Trust in Cancer Information Sources and Awareness of HPV as a Cause of Cervical Cancer Among US Adults — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: JAMA Netw Open
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-01 (EDAT: 2026-06-24)
- **Authors**: Oladoyin OO et al.
- **Aims**: Assess the association between trust in physicians, government health agencies, and religious organisations for cancer information and awareness that HPV causes cervical cancer.
- **Method**: Cross-sectional analysis of the nationally representative 2022 HINTS (Health Information National Trends Survey); N=3,914 US adults who had heard of HPV; multivariable logistic regression.
- **Key findings**: Little/no trust (vs. high trust) in government health agencies associated with lower HPV-cause awareness (aOR 0.68, 95% CI 0.49–0.95); non-Hispanic Asian adults had markedly lower awareness (aOR 0.31 vs. non-Hispanic White).
- **Strengths**: Nationally representative probability sample; HINTS data publicly available enabling replication; timely given declining institutional trust trends.
- **Limitations**: Cross-sectional design precludes causal inference; self-reported trust and HPV awareness; 2022 data may not reflect post-2023 trust dynamics.
- **Conclusion**: Erosion of trust in physicians and government health agencies threatens HPV/cervical cancer awareness, underscoring need for trust-building campaigns to reach cervical cancer elimination targets.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2026.20133 / PMID:42340719
