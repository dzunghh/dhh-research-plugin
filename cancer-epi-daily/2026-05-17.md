## Appraisal notes

This digest highlights a tension between methodological rigour and clinical novelty: the Milne JCO pooled analysis (#1) is the strongest evidence by design — 2.5 million participants across 30 cohorts — establishing male adiposity as a modifiable bladder cancer risk factor, with a clinically important sex difference that has mechanistic and screening implications. The Tatum GLP-1 paper (#2), despite severe observational limitations, generates the most provocative signal of the week: if even a fraction of the apparent survival benefit of GLP-1 RAs in breast cancer is real, it would transform the intersection of metabolic disease management and oncology. The Fox SEER-Medicare study (#3) delivers a sobering policy finding — the treatment gap in older adults with mNSCLC has been frozen for 15 years — underscoring that oncology progress is not reaching the oldest patients. Together, papers #4 and #5 frame systemic failures in both the cancer research funding pipeline and global surveillance of early-onset colorectal cancer.
