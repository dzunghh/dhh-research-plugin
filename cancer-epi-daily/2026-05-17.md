**1. Cancer Incidence and Mortality With Aspirin in Older Adults: Follow-Up of the ASPREE Trial** — *JAMA Oncology*, 2026-01-29 — Score 6/6
- **Authors:** Orchard SG et al.
- **Aims:** Assess long-term cancer incidence and mortality effects of low-dose aspirin vs placebo in older adults after ASPREE RCT
- **Method:** RCT follow-up; 19,114 adults ≥70 yr; Australia + US; median 8.6 yr; cancer registry linkage
- **Key findings:** Aspirin did not reduce cancer incidence; cancer mortality increased 15% (HR 1.15, 95% CI 1.03–1.29)
- **Strengths:** RCT design; multicountry; large n; registry-linked endpoints; long post-randomisation follow-up
- **Limitations:** Predominantly White participants; older adults only; post-unblinding observational phase limits causal certainty
- **Conclusion:** Low-dose aspirin should not be recommended for cancer prevention in healthy older adults
- **ID:** DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2025.6196

**2. Threshold Dose-Response Association Between Alcohol Consumption and Risk of Young-Onset Pancreatic Cancer: A Nationwide Korean Cohort Study** — *J Clin Oncol*, 2026-03-20 — Score 6/6
- **Authors:** Park JH et al.
- **Aims:** Characterise dose-response relationship between alcohol and early-onset pancreatic cancer in Korean adults aged 20–39 yr
- **Method:** Nationwide cohort; 6,263,770 Koreans age 20–39 yr; national health screening 2009–2012; registry follow-up to 2020
- **Key findings:** Heavy alcohol associated with young-onset pancreatic cancer in threshold dose-response pattern
- **Strengths:** Largest study of alcohol and early-onset pancreatic cancer; 6.26M participants; dose-response tested; registry-linked
- **Limitations:** Self-reported alcohol; Korean population only; short follow-up window; residual confounding possible
- **Conclusion:** Heavy drinking in young adults is a modifiable risk factor for early-onset pancreatic cancer
- **ID:** PMID:41576296 / DOI:10.1200/JCO-25-01169

**3. Rates of Systemic Treatment for Metastatic Non–Small Cell Lung Cancer Among Older Adults** — *JAMA Oncology*, 2026-05-07 — Score 5/6
- **Authors:** Fox AH et al.
- **Aims:** Characterise rates and temporal trends of systemic treatment receipt among older US adults with metastatic NSCLC
- **Method:** Population-based cohort; SEER-Medicare linkage; 254,611 patients ≥65 yr; metastatic NSCLC; 2006–2021
- **Key findings:** 46.8% received systemic treatment; 31% survived >90 days without any treatment; receipt rates rose over time
- **Strengths:** Large representative US registry; Medicare linkage enables treatment capture; 15-yr temporal trend; contemporary dataset

- **Conclusion:** Nearly half of older adults with metastatic NSCLC are untreated; barriers to care require urgent investigation
- **ID:** PMID:42096214 / DOI:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1080

## Appraisal notes

Today's digest highlights two themes: treatment/prevention paradoxes and unmet equity needs. The ASPREE trial (JAMA Oncology) provides definitive RCT evidence against aspirin for cancer prevention in older adults — a finding that reverses decades of observational optimism. The Korean cohort (J Clin Oncol) establishes a dose-response relationship between heavy alcohol use and early-onset pancreatic cancer in a massive nationwide dataset, adding a modifiable target. The Fox NSCLC study (JAMA Oncology) reveals a striking real-world treatment gap affecting half of older metastatic lung cancer patients in the US. The Lancet Commission on CRC and the Lancet Oncology equity paper together signal growing institutional recognition of the global and equity dimensions of cancer burden.
