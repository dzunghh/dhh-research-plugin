## 1. Estimates of Global Causes of Death for Children and Adolescents Aged 5–19 in 2000–24 — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: BMJ
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-04
- **Authors**: Eilerts-Spinelli H et al.
- **Aims**: Estimate cause-specific mortality in children/adolescents aged 5–19 across 195 countries from 2000 to 2024.
- **Method**: Secondary data analysis using Bayesian multinomial logistic regression on vital registration, disease surveillance, and modelled data.
- **Key findings**: Of 1.4 million global deaths in 5–19-year-olds in 2024, neoplasms ranked third (87,827 deaths; 90% UI 81,143–94,511). Progress in communicable diseases has slowed since 2015.
- **Strengths**: Large multi-source database (195 countries), rigorous Bayesian uncertainty quantification.
- **Limitations**: Modelled estimates for majority of countries; verbal autopsy data have variable quality.
- **Conclusion**: Cancer and NCDs increasingly dominate child/adolescent mortality as communicable disease burden declines.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1136/bmj-2025-088687 / PMID:42242773

## 2. The Cumulative Impact of Passenger Mutations on Cancer Development — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-04
- **Authors**: Nayak A et al.
- **Aims**: Assess whether passenger mutations collectively contribute to oncogenesis beyond the driver/passenger binary.
- **Method**: Cross-cohort analysis of >11,000 whole-genome-sequenced primary tumors across 3 cancer cohorts; enrichment, expression, and clinical outcome analyses.
- **Key findings**: Passenger mutations in cancer genes are enriched and show higher predicted pathogenicity when canonical drivers are absent. Accumulation in tumour-suppressor genes correlates with reduced expression and poorer prognosis.
- **Strengths**: Very large pan-cancer WGS cohort; multi-evidence framework (expression, splicing, TF binding, clinical outcomes).
- **Limitations**: Observational design; causal direction of passenger mutation accumulation vs. progression cannot be firmly established.
- **Conclusion**: Somatic mutation impact follows a continuum; integrative models incorporating all mutations better capture tumour evolution.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73930-3 / PMID:42243148

## 3. Integrative Genomic Analysis of 21 Orofacial Diseases Identifies Shared Genetic Architecture With Systemic Diseases — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-04
- **Authors**: Nam K et al.
- **Aims**: Map the genetic landscape of 21 orofacial diseases and clarify their causal relationships to systemic conditions including cancer.
- **Method**: UK Biobank GWAS, transcriptome-wide and rare-variant association studies, and Mendelian randomization; replicated in FinnGen.
- **Key findings**: 48 novel loci and 160 putative causal genes identified; MR supports a causal effect of Sjögren's syndrome on lung cancer (replicated in FinnGen). Dental caries/periodontitis share genetic architecture with nicotine pathways without robust cancer causality.
- **Strengths**: Multi-method triangulation (GWAS + TWAS + MR) with independent replication; CRISPR-Cas9 functional validation of key variant.
- **Limitations**: UK Biobank population may limit generalisability; MR assumptions (no horizontal pleiotropy) require validation.
- **Conclusion**: Sjögren's syndrome is causally linked to lung cancer risk, highlighting an overlooked immune-mediated cancer pathway.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73925-0 / PMID:42243103

## 4. Intrathecal Nivolumab in Metastatic Solid Tumors With Leptomeningeal Disease — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Nature Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-04
- **Authors**: Tabatabai G et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate safety, recommended dose, and preliminary survival of intraventricular nivolumab in solid tumour leptomeningeal metastatic disease (LMD).
- **Method**: Multicenter Phase 1 dose-escalation trial (IT-PD1/NOA-26); four dose levels (20–50 mg), IDSMC review between cohorts; n=24 intention-to-treat.
- **Key findings**: Primary safety endpoint met; recommended dose 50 mg. Median OS 6.6 months; 12-month OS 33.3% (95% CI 17.8–62.4%) in a historically dire-prognosis population.
- **Strengths**: Multicenter design, rigorous DSMB oversight, patient-reported quality-of-life data included.
- **Limitations**: Small sample (n=24 treated); single-arm without randomised control; LMD diagnosis heterogeneous across tumour types.
- **Conclusion**: Intraventricular nivolumab is safe and feasible for LMD, with survival signals warranting the ongoing expansion cohort.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s43018-026-01185-4 / PMID:42243276

## 5. Methylation-Based ctDNA Monitoring in Metastatic Breast Cancer During CDK4/6 Inhibitor Therapy — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Nature Communications
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-04
- **Authors**: Elliott MJ et al.
- **Aims**: Assess whether tissue-agnostic methylation tumour fraction (mTF) tracks treatment response and predicts outcomes in metastatic ER+/HER2− breast cancer.
- **Method**: Prospective observational study; 57 patients, 350 serial plasma timepoints; mTF compared with mutation-based ctDNA tracking and radiographic response.
- **Key findings**: Baseline ctDNA detectable in 94.7% of patients; mTF clearance associated with prolonged time to treatment discontinuation (HR=0.17, 95% CI 0.07–0.41). Molecular progression preceded clinical progression by median 5.8 months.
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive longitudinal sampling (350 timepoints); tissue-agnostic approach outperforms mutation-based tracking; clinical outcome validation.
- **Limitations**: Single-centre; small sample (n=57); no randomised ctDNA-guided intervention arm.
- **Conclusion**: Methylation-based ctDNA is a robust real-time treatment monitor in metastatic breast cancer, supporting prospective intervention trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41467-026-73126-9 / PMID:42243127
