## 1. Long-term effectiveness and overdiagnosis in the NHS breast screening programme: a cohort study with up to 33 years of follow-up — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Br J Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-26
- **Authors**: Dibden A et al.
- **Aims**: Assess long-term effectiveness and overdiagnosis of the NHS Breast Screening Programme with up to 33 years of follow-up.
- **Method**: Cohort study of 2,509,384 women aged 49–64; Poisson regression comparing screened vs uninvited; overdiagnosis estimated from excess diagnoses.
- **Key findings**: Invitation to screening reduced breast cancer mortality 28% (RR 0.72, 95% CI 0.71–0.74); overdiagnosis was low at 4% excess diagnoses (RR 1.04, 95% CI 1.02–1.07).
- **Strengths**: Largest and longest follow-up evaluation of NHS screening; population-based, 2.5M women, 33-year horizon.
- **Limitations**: Observational design; comparison group (uninvited) may differ systematically; screening status and confounders change over time.
- **Conclusion**: NHS breast screening remains effective in reducing mortality decades post-cessation with minimal overdiagnosis.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03493-z / PMID:42362870

## 2. Tumour-based DNA methylation markers of breast cancer survival: a pooled analysis of 2157 cases — Score 6/6

- **Journal**: Breast Cancer Res
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-26
- **Authors**: Zarean E et al.
- **Aims**: Identify tumour DNA methylation markers associated with breast cancer survival and develop a prognostic epigenetic signature.
- **Method**: EWAS of 2,157 breast tumours across 10 datasets; elastic net Cox regression developed a 228-CpG signature; validated in MCCS (N=425) and triple-negative subgroup.
- **Key findings**: 228-CpG signature improved 5-year overall survival C-index from 0.75 to 0.79 (increase 0.04, 95% CI 0.01–0.10); per-SD HR=1.7 (95% CI 1.2–2.3).
- **Strengths**: Large pooled EWAS sample; independent validation across multiple datasets and triple-negative replication set.
- **Limitations**: Retrospective; methylation assays not standardised across contributing datasets; prospective clinical utility unproven.
- **Conclusion**: Tumour DNA methylation provides modest but significant prognostic information beyond standard clinicopathological factors.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1186/s13058-026-02327-3 / PMID:42363280

## 3. Ovarian cancer tumor immune profiles associated with intrauterine device and oral contraceptive use — Score 5/6

- **Journal**: Br J Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-27
- **Authors**: Mongiovi JM et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate associations of IUD and oral contraceptive use with ovarian tumour immune infiltration.
- **Method**: Pooled analysis of 6 studies (4 case-control, 2 cohort); multiplex immunofluorescence on 24 tumour tissue microarrays; multivariable beta-binomial models.
- **Key findings**: IUD use modestly associated with increased cytotoxic T cell infiltration (CD3+CD8+ OR 1.14, 95% CI 0.99–1.32); OC use ≥1yr associated with lower cytotoxic T cell odds (OR 0.89, 95% CI 0.79–1.00).
- **Strengths**: Multi-study pooled design with granular T cell subtyping via multiplex immunofluorescence across 6 studies.
- **Limitations**: Key estimates have CIs crossing the null; contraceptive exposure assessed retrospectively; causal direction uncertain.
- **Conclusion**: Pre-diagnostic contraception modulates ovarian tumour immune infiltration, potentially affecting cancer susceptibility via immune mechanisms.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03508-9 / PMID:42365137

## 4. Metabolic Modulation in Cancer Care: The Potential Role of Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor Agonists — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: J Clin Oncol
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-29
- **Authors**: Temperley HC et al.
- **Aims**: Synthesise epidemiologic and mechanistic evidence for GLP-1RA use in cancer prevention and treatment.
- **Method**: Comprehensive narrative review integrating epidemiology, mechanistic studies, and clinical evidence for GLP-1RAs across obesity-related cancer types.
- **Key findings**: GLP-1RA therapy linked to lower risk of GI, breast, endometrial, ovarian, prostate, and haematologic malignancies; mechanisms include anti-inflammatory, anti-angiogenic, and immune modulation.
- **Strengths**: Timely and comprehensive synthesis given widespread GLP-1RA uptake; covers epidemiology through mechanistic pathways to clinical application.
- **Limitations**: Narrative review; epidemiologic evidence largely observational with confounding by obesity and metabolic comorbidities.
- **Conclusion**: GLP-1RAs show promise as metabolically targeted agents for cancer prevention and adjunct treatment, warranting prospective randomised trials.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1200/JCO-26-00062 / PMID:42372204

## 5. Anti-PDGFRα autoantibody — a novel diagnostic and prognostic marker — may mediate non-small cell lung cancer progression via the PI3K/AKT/NF-κB signaling pathway — Score 4/6

- **Journal**: Br J Cancer
- **Pub date**: 2026-06-27
- **Authors**: Chen F et al.
- **Aims**: Evaluate anti-PDGFRα autoantibody as a diagnostic and prognostic biomarker in non-small cell lung cancer.
- **Method**: ELISA in 1,170 plasma samples for diagnostic AUC; Cox regression in 353 NSCLC cases for prognostic value; in-vitro cell line experiments for mechanistic validation.
- **Key findings**: Anti-PDGFRα AUC 0.747 (95% CI 0.701–0.794) for early NSCLC diagnosis; independent poor-prognosis predictor (HR 1.510, 95% CI 1.094–2.098).

- **Limitations**: Single-centre study; limited independent replication; in-vitro mechanism may not fully reflect in-vivo biology.
- **Conclusion**: Anti-PDGFRα autoantibody is a promising early diagnostic and prognostic biomarker in NSCLC warranting multicentre validation.
- **ID**: DOI:10.1038/s41416-026-03501-2 / PMID:42365136

## Appraisal notes

This week's digest is anchored by two strong breast-cancer cohort studies: the 33-year NHS screening evaluation (PMID 42362870) stands out for its rare combination of scale and duration, offering among the most definitive long-term estimates of mammographic screening effectiveness and overdiagnosis to date. Alongside it, the 2,157-case pooled EWAS for DNA methylation prognostic signatures (PMID 42363280) reflects growing momentum in epigenomic cancer epidemiology, moving from discovery to validated prediction models. A broader thematic signal is the convergence of metabolic epidemiology and oncology, exemplified by the GLP-1RA review in J Clin Oncol (PMID 42372204), consistent with rising interest in obesity-pathway interventions as cancer-modifying agents.
